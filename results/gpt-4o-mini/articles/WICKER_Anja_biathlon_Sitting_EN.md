# Anja Wicker: A Trailblazer in Paralympic Biathlon

## Introduction
Anja Wicker is a German athlete specializing in biathlon, competing in the LW10 classification for sitting athletes. Born on December 22, 1991, Wicker has made significant contributions to the sport, particularly through her performances at the Paralympic Games and World Championships.

## Career Overview
Anja Wicker's career in biathlon includes participation in the following events:

- **2014 Sochi Winter Paralympics**: Achieved success.
- **2018 PyeongChang Winter Paralympics**: Faced challenges but continued to compete.
- **2021 World Para Snow Sports Championships**: Achieved multiple medals.
- **2022 Beijing Winter Paralympics**: Showcased her skills with strong performances.
- **Upcoming 2026 Milano Cortina Winter Paralympics**: Set to compete again.

In addition to biathlon, Wicker also participates in cross-country skiing.

## Major Achievements
Anja Wicker's career is highlighted by numerous accolades, including:

- **2014 Sochi Winter Paralympics**:
  - Gold Medal: Women's 10 km Sitting
  - Silver Medal: Women's 12.5 km Sitting
- **2022 Beijing Winter Paralympics**:
  - Bronze Medal: Women's Middle Distance Sitting
  - 4th Place: Women's Individual Sitting
  - 5th Place: Women's Sprint Sitting
- **2021 World Para Snow Sports Championships**:
  - Gold Medal: Women's 6 km Sitting
  - Bronze Medal: Women's 7.5 km Sitting
- **Overall World Cup Titles**:
  - Winner in Biathlon for multiple seasons: 2014/15, 2016/17, 2020/21, 2023/24, 2024/25, 2025/26.

## Performance Trends
- Consistent podium finishes in major events, including multiple medals at the Paralympics and World Championships.

## Other Sports
Anja Wicker also competes in cross-country skiing, with notable achievements including:

- **2025 World Championships**:
  - Gold Medal: 10 km Cross-Country

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Anja Wicker's training regimen includes:

- Training begins annually on May 1, with a commitment of 15-20 hours per week.
- Summer training includes roller skiing and strength sessions at the Olympic training center in Stuttgart.
- Family involvement includes her father as her coach and her mother managing her nutrition.

## Notable Facts
- Anja Wicker was born with Caudal Regression Syndrome, which affects her lower body, leading to her classification as a sitting athlete.
