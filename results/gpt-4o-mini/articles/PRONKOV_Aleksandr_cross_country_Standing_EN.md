# Aleksandr Pronkov: A Profile of a Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing Champion

## Introduction
Aleksandr Pronkov is a Russian athlete competing in cross-country skiing. Born on May 29, 1997, he participates in the LW5/7 classification, which is designated for standing athletes.

## Career Overview
Aleksandr Pronkov's career in cross-country skiing includes participation in the 2014 Paralympic Winter Games held in Sochi. He has participated in various international competitions, including World Championships and World Cups.

## Major Achievements
- **Gold Medal** in Men's 10 km Standing at the 2014 Winter Paralympics.
- **Silver Medal** in Men's Sprint Standing at the Lillehammer 2021 World Para Snow Sports Championships.
- **Bronze Medal** in Men's Middle Distance - Classic Standing at the Lillehammer 2021 World Para Snow Sports Championships.
- **4th Place** in Men's 12.5 km Standing at the 2014 Winter Paralympics.
- **4th Place** in Men's 1 km Sprint Standing at the 2014 Winter Paralympics.
- **6th Place** in Men's 15 km Standing at the 2014 Winter Paralympics.
- **10th Place** in Men's 7.5 km Standing at the 2014 Winter Paralympics.

## Performance Trends
- Consistent podium finishes in World Championships and World Cups, particularly in sprint and middle-distance events.
