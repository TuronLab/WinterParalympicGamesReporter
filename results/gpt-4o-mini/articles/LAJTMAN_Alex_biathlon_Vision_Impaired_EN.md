# The Rising Star of Slovak Para Sports: Alex Lajtman

## Introduction
Alex Lajtman is a para-athlete from Slovakia, specializing in biathlon and cross-country skiing. Born on September 20, 2003, Lajtman is preparing for the 2026 Winter Paralympics in Milan and Cortina d'Ampezzo.

## Career Overview
Alex Lajtman's athletic journey began with alpine skiing. He transitioned to cross-country skiing and biathlon. He is the only Slovak representative in para cross-country skiing.

## Major Achievements
- Qualified for the **2026 Winter Paralympics** in Milan and Cortina d'Ampezzo.
- Secured **third place** in the **European Cup** in Croatia.

## Performance Trends
- Currently undergoing intensive training focused on both cross-country skiing and biathlon skills.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2026 Winter Paralympics**: Competing in Cross-Country Skiing (sprint, 10 km, 20 km); aims to qualify for Biathlon.

## Other Sports
- **Alpine Skiing**: 
  - Achieved **third place** in the **European Cup** in Croatia.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Alex trains alongside a guide, Marko Havran, a former competitive cross-country skier, due to his limited vision. This method allows him to navigate trails using vocal instructions.

## Notable Facts
- Alex has limited sight due to nerve damage he sustained at the age of three, which permits him to see only up to 2.5 meters.
- His sporting potential was discovered through various camps organized by the Slovak Paralympic Committee.
