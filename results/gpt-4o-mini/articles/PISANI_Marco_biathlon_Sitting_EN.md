# Marco Pisani: A Rising Star in Paralympic Biathlon

## Introduction
Marco Pisani is an Italian Paralympic athlete specializing in biathlon, competing in the sitting category. Born on June 7, 1988, in Bagno a Ripoli, Italy, Pisani is set to represent Italy at the 2026 Milano Cortina Paralympic Winter Games.

## Career Overview
Marco Pisani's career in sports began after he lost his left leg during a military operation in Iraq in 2019. He transitioned to competitive sports, focusing on biathlon and Nordic skiing. 

## Major Achievements
- **Biathlon:**
  - Gold medal in Biathlon Sit Ski at the Invictus Games 2025.
  - Participation in the 2026 Milano Cortina Paralympic Winter Games.

## Performance Trends
- Competed in multiple World Cup events in biathlon, achieving placements ranging from 8th to 13th.
- Notable performances include:
  - 12th in the 7.5 km sprint at Notschrei (GER) - 2026.
  - 8th in the sprint pursuit at Notschrei (GER) - 2026.
  - 13th in the sprint pursuit at Szklarska Poreba Jakuszyce (POL) - 2026.
  - 13th in the 12.5 km Individual at Szklarska Poreba Jakuszyce (POL) - 2026.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2026 Milano Cortina Paralympic Winter Games**
  - Sport: Biathlon

## Other Sports
- **Nordic Skiing:**
  - 26th in sprint at Val di Fiemme (ITA) - 2025.
  - 17th in sprint at Vuokatti (FIN) - 2024.
  - 24th in sprint at Martell (ITA) - 2024.
  - 14th in 10 km Interval Start at Continental Cup in Galà (NOR) - 2023.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Marco Pisani engages in extensive summer training, which includes handbike and ski roll sessions to prepare for his competitions.
