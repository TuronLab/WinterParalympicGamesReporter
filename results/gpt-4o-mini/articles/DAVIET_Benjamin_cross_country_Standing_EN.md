# Benjamin Daviet: A Trailblazer in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Benjamin Daviet is a French athlete specializing in cross-country skiing, classified under the LW2 category. Born on June 16, 1989, Daviet has made significant contributions to the Paralympic sports arena, particularly through his impressive performances in the Winter Paralympics. His journey in adaptive sports began after a life-changing accident at the age of 17.

## Career Overview
Daviet's career in cross-country skiing has been marked by a series of remarkable achievements since he began competing at the international level. His trajectory can be summarized as follows:

- **2014**: Made his Paralympic debut at the Sochi Winter Games, where he secured a bronze medal in the 4x2.5 km Open Relay.
- **2018**: Achieved a stellar performance at the PyeongChang Winter Paralympics, winning three gold medals (4x2.5 km Open Relay, 12.5 km Standing) and two silver medals (15 km Standing, 20 km Free Standing).
- **2022**: Continued his success at the Beijing Winter Paralympics, earning a gold medal in the 1.5 km Standing and two silver medals (12.5 km Free Standing, 4x2.5 km Open Relay).

In addition to his cross-country skiing career, Daviet has also participated in para rowing, where he has been preparing for the Paris 2024 Paralympics.

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing**:
  - **2014 Sochi**: Bronze in 4x2.5 km Open Relay.
  - **2018 PyeongChang**:
    - Gold in 4x2.5 km Open Relay.
    - Gold in 12.5 km Standing.
    - Silver in 15 km Standing.
    - Silver in 20 km Free Standing.
  - **2022 Beijing**:
    - Gold in 1.5 km Standing.
    - Silver in 12.5 km Free Standing.
    - Silver in 4x2.5 km Open Relay.
- **Overall**: 10 Paralympic medals (5 Gold, 4 Silver, 1 Bronze).
- **World Championships**: 9-time World Champion in Para Biathlon and Cross-Country.

## Performance Trends
- Consistent podium finishes in major events since 2014.
- Dominated the 2018 Winter Paralympics with multiple medals.
- Continued success in World Championships and World Cups.
