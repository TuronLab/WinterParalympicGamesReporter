# Oksana Masters: A Multifaceted Paralympic Champion

## Introduction
Oksana Masters is a distinguished athlete from the United States, specializing in biathlon and competing in the Women's Sitting category. She has made significant contributions to Paralympic sports, showcasing her talents across multiple disciplines, including Para Nordic skiing, Para-cycling, and Para-rowing. Masters has participated in numerous international competitions.

## Career Overview
Oksana Masters began her athletic career in rowing, where she won a gold medal in trunk and arms mixed double sculls at the 2012 Summer Paralympics. In 2014, she transitioned to Para Nordic skiing and biathlon. She has continued to excel in both biathlon and Para Nordic skiing, earning multiple medals. She achieved significant success at the Beijing 2022 Paralympic Games and is set to compete in her seventh Paralympic Games in 2024.

## Major Achievements
Oksana Masters has an impressive list of accomplishments, including:

- **Biathlon**:
  - Silver medal in the 6 km sitting biathlon event at the 2021 World Para Snow Sports Championships (Source: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oksana_Masters)).
  - Gold medal in the Women's Sitting 12.5 km Individual at the IBU World Cup (January 2026) (Source: [US Para Nordic](https://www.usparanordic.org/news/2026/january/12/oksana-masters-returns-to-the-top-of-the-podium-at-ibu-world-cup-notschrei)).
  - 3 Golds and 4 Silvers at the Beijing 2022 Paralympic Games (Source: [Toyota Times](https://toyotatimes.jp/en/sports/teams_athletes/athlete/oksana_masters.html)).
  - Multiple medals at the World Para Nordic Ski Championships (Source: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oksana_Masters)).
  
- **Other Sports**:
  - Gold medal in trunk and arms mixed double sculls at the 2012 Summer Paralympics (Source: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oksana_Masters)).

## Performance Trends
- Consistent medal performances in major competitions, including World Championships and Paralympic Games (Source: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oksana_Masters)).

## Paralympic Participations
Oksana Masters has participated in the following Paralympic events:

- **2012**: Para-rowing, Gold medal in trunk and arms mixed double sculls.
- **2014**: Winter Paralympics, competed in biathlon and Para Nordic skiing.
- **2016**: Winter Paralympics, continued to compete in biathlon and Para Nordic skiing.
- **2018**: Winter Paralympics, achieved multiple medals.
- **2020**: Summer Paralympics, competed in Para-cycling.
- **2022**: Winter Paralympics, 3 Golds and 4 Silvers (Source: [Toyota Times](https://toyotatimes.jp/en/sports/teams_athletes/athlete/oksana_masters.html)).
- **2024**: Scheduled to compete in her seventh Paralympic Games.

## Other Sports
In addition to biathlon, Oksana Masters has participated in:

- **Para Nordic Skiing**: Achieved multiple medals at World Championships (Source: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oksana_Masters)).
- **Para-cycling**: Competed in the 2020 Summer Paralympics.
- **Para-rowing**: Gold medal in trunk and arms mixed double sculls at the 2012 Summer Paralympics (Source: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oksana_Masters)).

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Oksana Masters employs a comprehensive training regimen that balances her preparation for skiing and cycling. She often incorporates cycling practice into her routine (Source: [Self](https://www.self.com/story/oksana-masters)).

## Notable Facts
- Oksana Masters was born on June 19, 1989, in Khmelnitsky, Ukraine, and later moved to the United States (Source: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oksana_Masters)).
- She is a 19-time Paralympic medalist and a 28-time World Championships medalist (Source: [Women's Sports Foundation](https://www.womenssportsfoundation.org/athlete/oksana-masters/)).
