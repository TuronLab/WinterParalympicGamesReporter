# Sydney Peterson: A Rising Star in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Sydney Peterson is a distinguished athlete from the United States, competing in cross-country skiing. She participates in events designed for athletes with disabilities. Peterson's journey in the world of Paralympic sports has been marked by significant achievements, particularly at the 2022 Beijing Paralympics, where she won Gold, Silver, and Bronze medals.

## Career Overview
Sydney Peterson began her skiing journey at the age of five, initially using the sport as a form of physical therapy. Diagnosed with dystonia, which affects her left arm and leg, she adapted her technique to ski with one pole. Peterson's career trajectory has been characterized by her commitment to both her sport and her academic pursuits, as she balances her training with doctoral research in neuroscience at the University of Utah, focusing on movement disorders.
