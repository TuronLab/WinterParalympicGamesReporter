# Miroslav Motejzik: A Trailblazer in Paralympic Winter Sports

## Introduction
Miroslav Motejzik is a Czech athlete specializing in cross-country skiing and para biathlon. Competing in the LW4 classification (Standing), Motejzik has participated in para sports since 2010, following a car accident that resulted in the amputation of his left leg below the knee.

## Career Overview
Motejzik's career trajectory reflects a commitment to excellence across multiple disciplines:
- **2010**: Began participating in para sports after his accident.
- **2021**: Achieved the title of XTERRA Maui World Champion in off-road triathlon.
- **2023**: Completed the Nordic skiing Grand Slam, which includes events such as Marcialonga, Jizerská 50, Vasaloppet, and Birkebeinerrennet.
- **2025**: Competed as the first Czech para biathlete in Val di Fiemme and Pokljuka.
- **2026**: Participated in various cross-country skiing events, including the FIS competitions in Szklarska Poreba-Jakuszyce and Finsterau.

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing**:
  - Completed the Nordic skiing Grand Slam in 2023.
  - First disabled athlete to complete the 220 km Nordenskiöldsloppet on skis (double poling).

- **Triathlon**:
  - 2021 XTERRA Maui World Champion in off-road triathlon.
