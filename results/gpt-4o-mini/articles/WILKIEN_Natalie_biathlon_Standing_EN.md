# Natalie Wilkie: A Rising Star in Paralympic Winter Sports

## Introduction
Natalie Wilkie is a Canadian Paralympic athlete specializing in biathlon and cross-country skiing. Competing in the standing category (LW8), she has made significant strides in her sports career. Wilkie's journey in para sports began after a life-altering accident in 2016.

## Career Overview
Natalie Wilkie's career trajectory has been marked by rapid development and impressive achievements in both biathlon and cross-country skiing. 

- **2016**: After losing four fingers in a woodworking accident, Wilkie transitioned to para sports.
- **2018**: Made her Paralympic debut at the PyeongChang Winter Paralympics.
- **2022**: Competed in the Beijing Winter Paralympics.
- **2025-26 Season**: Achieved six victories on the Para biathlon World Cup circuit.

## Major Achievements
- **Biathlon**:
  - Double biathlon World Champion.
  - Gold medal in the women's 7.5 km sprint at the World Cup in Vuokatti.
  - Consistent podium finishes in various World Cup events.
  
- **Cross-Country Skiing**:
  - Three-time Paralympic gold medalist.
  - Medals at the 2018 and 2022 Winter Paralympics.

## Performance Trends
- In the **2025-26 season**, Wilkie collected six victories on the Para biathlon World Cup circuit.
- Achieved a podium finish in her first biathlon race of the season despite recovering from illness.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2018 Winter Paralympics**: Competed in cross-country skiing, winning multiple medals.
- **2022 Winter Paralympics**: Continued her success in cross-country skiing.

## Other Sports
- **Cross-Country Skiing**:
  - Three-time Paralympic gold medalist.
  - Achievements at the 2018 and 2022 Winter Paralympics.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Natalie Wilkie's training regimen is tailored to her dual focus on biathlon and cross-country skiing. She adjusts her training based on competition schedules. Additionally, she engages in shooting practice to enhance her accuracy and speed.

## Notable Facts
- Born on January 21, 2001, in Salmon Arm, British Columbia, Canada.
- Lost four fingers on her left hand in a woodworking accident in 2016.
