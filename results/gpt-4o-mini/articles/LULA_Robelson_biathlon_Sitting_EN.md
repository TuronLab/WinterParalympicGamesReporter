# Robelson Lula: A Rising Star in Paralympic Biathlon

## Introduction
Robelson Moreira Lula, born on May 4, 1993, is a Brazilian athlete specializing in biathlon, competing in the LW12 classification for sitting athletes. Lula made his mark on the international stage during the Beijing 2022 Paralympic Winter Games, where he represented Brazil in both biathlon and cross-country skiing.

## Career Overview
Robelson Lula's athletic journey began in swimming, but he later transitioned to wheelchair handball and athletics before focusing on cross-country skiing and biathlon. He participated in the World Cup for cross-country skiing in 2018 in Vuokatti, Finland.

## Major Achievements
- **2022 Paralympic Winter Games (Beijing)**
  - Men's Long Distance Sitting: 20th place (March 6, 2022)
  - Men's Sprint Sitting: 21st place (March 9, 2022)
  - Men's Middle Distance Sitting: 20th place (March 12, 2022)
  - Mixed 4x2.5km Relay: 8th place (March 13, 2022)
- Ranked as the third best Brazilian in the world ranking for his category.

## Performance Trends

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Robelson Lula has participated in training camps in Germany with the Brazilian Para Biathlon team, focusing on skiing techniques and shooting skills. His preparation also includes rollerskiing.

## Notable Facts
- Robelson Lula lost his right leg above the knee due to cancer at the age of 12.
- He began his sports career in São Carlos, São Paulo, after his amputation.
