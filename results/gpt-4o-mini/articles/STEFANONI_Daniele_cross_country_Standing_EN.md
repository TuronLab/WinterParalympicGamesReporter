# Daniele Stefanoni: A Profile of Resilience and Dedication in Paralympic Sports

## Introduction
Daniele Stefanoni is an accomplished Italian athlete specializing in cross-country skiing. Born on August 28, 1966, he competes in the LW4 classification, which is designated for standing athletes. Stefanoni has a history of participation in both winter and summer Paralympic events.

## Career Overview
Daniele Stefanoni's athletic career spans multiple sports, primarily focusing on cross-country skiing and rowing. His journey includes:

- **Rowing:** Competed in the trunk and arms mixed double sculls (TAMix2x) at the World Rowing Championships and participated in the Paralympic Games.
- **Cross-Country Skiing:** Actively competing in World Cup events leading up to the 2026 Winter Paralympics.

## Major Achievements
- **Paralympic Games:**
  - 2006 Winter Paralympics (Cross-Country Skiing)
  - 2008 Summer Paralympics (Rowing) - Finished fourth in the trunk and arms mixed double sculls (TAMix2x).
  - 2012 Summer Paralympics (Rowing)

- **World Championships and World Cup:**
  - Participation in various World Cup events with notable placements:
    - 11th place in Mass Start, Szklarska Poreba-Jakuszyce, Poland (World Cup, 01-02-2026)
    - 14th place in Interval Start, Szklarska Poreba-Jakuszyce, Poland (World Cup, 31-01-2026)
    - 15th place in Open Relay, Finsterau, Germany (World Cup, 18-01-2026)
    - 27th place in Interval Start, Toblach, Italy (World Championships, 14-02-2025)
    - 23rd place in Interval Start, Toblach, Italy (World Championships, 12-02-2025)
