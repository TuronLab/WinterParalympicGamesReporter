# DEKIJIMA Momoko: A Profile of Resilience and Achievement in Paralympic Winter Sports

## Introduction
DEKIJIMA Momoko is a distinguished Japanese athlete specializing in cross-country skiing and biathlon. Competing in the LW6 classification (Standing), she has made significant contributions to the Paralympic sports scene. Her participation spans five Paralympic Winter Games.

## Career Overview
DEKIJIMA's career in winter sports began after she took up skiing following her recovery from surgery to remove a tumor. She has participated in both cross-country skiing and biathlon.

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing:**
  - Competed in five Paralympic Winter Games (2006, 2010, 2014, 2018, 2022).
  - 14th in Women's 7.5 km Classic - Standing at the 2022 Paralympic Winter Games (Beijing).
  - 12th in Women's 12.5 km - Standing at the 2022 Paralympic Winter Games (Beijing).
  - 10th in Women's 10 km - Standing at the 2022 Paralympic Winter Games (Beijing).
  - 14th in Women's 6 km - Standing at the 2022 Paralympic Winter Games (Beijing).
  - 4th in Mixed Relay 4x2.5 km at the 2018 Paralympic Winter Games (Pyeongchang).
  - 12th in Women's 7.5 km Classic - Standing at the 2018 Paralympic Winter Games (Pyeongchang).
  - 9th in Women's 12.5 km - Standing at the 2018 Paralympic Winter Games (Pyeongchang).
  - 9th in Women's 10 km - Standing at the 2018 Paralympic Winter Games (Pyeongchang).
  - 7th in Women's 15 km Free - Standing at the 2018 Paralympic Winter Games (Pyeongchang).
  - 13th in Women's 6 km - Standing at the 2018 Paralympic Winter Games (Pyeongchang).
  
- **Biathlon:**
  - Silver medal at the 2017 IPC Nordic Skiing World Championships in Biathlon.
  - 5th place in Biathlon at the 2021/2022 season.
  - 14th place in Women's 7.5 km Classic - Standing at the 2021/2022 Paralympic Winter Games.
