# Johanna Recktenwald: A Rising Star in Para Cross-Country Skiing and Biathlon

## Introduction
Johanna Recktenwald is a prominent German athlete competing in Para Cross-Country Skiing and Biathlon, classified under the B2 category, which accommodates athletes with visual impairments. Born on June 11, 2001, Recktenwald has made significant strides in her sport, representing Germany at international levels, including the Winter Paralympics and World Para Nordic Skiing Championships.

## Career Overview
Recktenwald's athletic career began in her youth, focusing on cross-country skiing and biathlon. 

### Sports and Categories Participated In:
- **Para Cross-Country Skiing:** B2 (Visually Impaired)
- **Para Biathlon:** Concurrent with her cross-country skiing efforts
- **Para Cycling:** Competing in tandem cycling as a separate endeavor.

## Major Achievements
Johanna Recktenwald has amassed an impressive list of achievements throughout her career:

- **Cross-Country Skiing:**
  - **2022 Winter Paralympics:** 4th place in both 10 km and 12.5 km events.
  - **2023 World Para Nordic Skiing Championships:**
    - Silver in the 12.5 km Individual
    - Bronze in the 7.5 km Sprint
    - Bronze in Sprint Freestyle
  - **2025 Winter World University Games:** Silver medals in:
    - Sprint Classical
    - 10 km Freestyle

- **Para Biathlon:**
  - **2025 Biathlon World Championships:** Gold medalist.
  - **2025:** Awarded "Para-Sportlerin des Jahres" in Germany.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2022 Winter Paralympics (Beijing):**  
  - Sport: Para Cross-Country Skiing  
  - Events: 10 km (4th place), 12.5 km (4th place)
  
- **Future Participation:**
  - Selected to represent Germany in the **2026 Winter Paralympics**.

## Other Sports
Johanna Recktenwald is involved in para cycling alongside her primary sports:

- **Para Cycling (Tandem Cycling):**
  - Achieved a Gold medal at the **2025 Biathlon World Championships**.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Recktenwald trains with the Biathlon Team Saarland and utilizes the Olympic training base in Freiburg. The preparation focuses on mastering courses specifically designed for visually impaired competitors.

## Notable Facts
- Hometown: Marpingen, Saarland; currently residing in Freiburg im Breisgau.
- She is pursuing studies in health education at PH Freiburg.
