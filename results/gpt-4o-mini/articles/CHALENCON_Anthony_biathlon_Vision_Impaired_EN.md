# Anthony Chalençon: A Beacon in Para Biathlon

## Introduction
Anthony Chalençon is a French athlete specializing in biathlon and cross-country skiing. Born on August 13, 1990, Chalençon competes in the visually impaired category, specifically B1, due to retinal degeneration. 

## Career Overview
Chalençon's athletic journey began as an alpine skier, where he competed until the Vancouver 2010 Paralympics. He transitioned into Nordic skiing and biathlon after that.

## Major Achievements
- **2018 Winter Paralympics:**
  - **Gold Medal** in Open Relay (4 x 2.5 km)
  - **Bronze Medal** in 15 km Visually Impaired
- **World Championships:**
  - Two-time World Champion in Relay (2015, 2017)
  - **Bronze Medal** in the 2017 World Championships (7.5 km Sprint)

### Titles Won
- Paralympic Gold (2018)
- Paralympic Bronze (2018)
- Multiple titles across World Championships and World Cups.

## Performance Trends
- Competed consistently in various distances in visually impaired events.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2010 Paralympic Winter Games:** Competitor in alpine skiing (no significant medals attributed).
- **2018 Paralympic Winter Games:**
  - Sport: Biathlon
  - Result: Gold in Open Relay, Bronze in 15 km Visually Impaired
- **2022 Paralympic Winter Games (Beijing):**
  - Sport: Biathlon
  - Result: Silver in Open Relay

## Other Sports

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Information regarding Chalençon's training methods and preparation style is not publicly available. He currently trains with guide Brice Ottonello.
