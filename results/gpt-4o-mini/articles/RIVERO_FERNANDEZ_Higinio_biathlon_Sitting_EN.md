# Higinio Rivero Fernández: A Trailblazer in Paralympic Winter Sports

## Introduction
Higinio Rivero Fernández is a Spanish athlete specializing in biathlon and para-canoeing. He competes in the LW 10.5 category, which is designated for athletes with spinal cord injuries competing in a seated position. 

## Career Overview
Higinio Rivero's participation in biathlon began with a focus on accumulating points for Paralympic qualification, leading to his debut in the 2025 World Championships. In addition to biathlon, Rivero has also competed in para-canoeing, participating in the Tokyo 2020 and Paris 2024 Paralympic Games.

## Major Achievements
- **Biathlon:**
  - First Spanish athlete to compete in a Paralympic Biathlon World Championship (2025) (source: Marca).
  - Qualified for the Milano-Cortina 2026 Paralympic Games in both biathlon and cross-country skiing (source: Jaca Times).
  - 21st in the 7.5 km sprint at the 2025 World Championships (source: Marca).
  - 22nd in the pursuit-sprint at the 2025 World Championships (source: Marca).
  - 20th in the 12.5 km event at the 2025 World Championships (source: Marca).

- **Para-Canoeing:**
  - Competed in para-canoeing events at the Tokyo 2020 and Paris 2024 Paralympic Games (source: Marca).
  - Aiming for the European Championships in June 2026 (source: Marca).

## Performance Trends
- Competed in multiple World Cup events in Finland, Italy, Norway, Sweden, and Slovenia during the 2024-25 season, focusing on accumulating points for Paralympic qualification (source: Jaca Times).

## Paralympic Participations
- **Tokyo 2020 Paralympic Games**: Competed in para-canoeing (source: Marca).
- **Paris 2024 Paralympic Games**: Competed in para-canoeing (source: Marca).

## Other Sports
- **Para-Canoeing:**
  - Competed in para-canoeing events at the Tokyo 2020 and Paris 2024 Paralympic Games (source: Marca).
  - Aiming for the European Championships in June 2026 (source: Marca).

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
- Trains at the Technical Perfection Center in Fadura, utilizing specialized equipment and roller skis to simulate competitive conditions (source: Marca).
- Engages in mental training to enhance shooting performance under physical exertion (source: Marca).

## Notable Facts
- Born in 1982 in Barakaldo, Spain, Rivero suffered a spinal cord injury in 2013 due to a climbing accident, which led to his transition into adaptive sports (source: El Plural).
- Represents the Club Pirineísta Mayencos and the Aragonese Federation of Winter Sports (source: Jaca Times).
