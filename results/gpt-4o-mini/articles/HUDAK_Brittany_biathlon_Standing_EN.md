# Brittany Hudak: A Trailblazer in Para-Nordic Skiing

## Introduction
Brittany Hudak is a Canadian para-nordic skier born on June 2, 1993, in Prince Albert, Saskatchewan. Competing in the LW6/8 classification, she has participated in multiple Paralympic Games.

## Career Overview
Brittany Hudak's career in para-nordic skiing began when she was introduced to the sport by Colette Bourgonje at the age of 18. Hudak has been a member of the Senior National Team for nine years.

## Major Achievements
- **2018 Winter Paralympics**: Bronze medal in Women's 12.5 km biathlon.
- **2022 Winter Paralympics**: 
  - Bronze medal in Women's 12.5 km biathlon.
  - Bronze medal in Women's 15 km classical standing.
- **Para Nordic World Cup (December 2021)**: Gold medal in Canmore.
- **2014-15**: World Cup Overall Cross-Country Champion.

## Performance Trends
- Achieved notable success during the 2021-2022 World Para Nordic Skiing World Cup season, securing four gold medals and one silver, including two golds in biathlon events.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2014 Winter Paralympics**: Participated.
- **2018 Winter Paralympics**: Bronze in Women's 12.5 km biathlon.
- **2022 Winter Paralympics**: 
  - Bronze in Women's 12.5 km biathlon.
  - Bronze in Women's 15 km classical standing.

## Other Sports
- **Cross-Country Skiing**: 
  - Bronze in Women's 15 km classical standing at the 2022 Winter Paralympics.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach

## Notable Facts
- Brittany was born with a disability, missing part of her left arm below the elbow.
- She holds a Bachelor’s Degree in Social Work from the University of Regina.
