# Dave Miln: A Journey of Resilience in Paralympic Sports

## Introduction
Dave Miln is an Australian athlete competing in Para Cross-Country Skiing and Para Biathlon. Classified as an LW12 sit skier, Miln is set to represent Australia at the 2026 Winter Paralympics. His journey into adaptive sports began after a skiing accident in 2022, which resulted in the amputation of both legs.

## Career Overview
- **2024**: Miln first competed for Australia in Para Cross-Country Skiing and Para Biathlon in Canada.
- **2024/25 Season**: He began participating in FIS international events.
- **2025**: Competed at the Invictus Games, where he achieved notable success.
- **2026**: Selected to represent Australia at the Winter Paralympics in both biathlon and cross-country skiing.

## Major Achievements
- **2025 Invictus Games**: 
  - Silver medal in advanced biathlon.
  - Served as the flag bearer for the Australian team.
- **2026 Winter Paralympics**: 
  - Selected to compete in both Para Cross-Country Skiing and Para Biathlon.

## Performance Trends
- Transitioned from an upright skier to a sit-skier following his injuries.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Miln emphasizes a military mindset in his training, focusing on perseverance through adversity. His preparation involves adapting techniques from his previous experience as an upright skier to his current classification as a sit-skier.

## Notable Facts
- Born on December 6, 1985, in Salisbury, England.
- Served nearly 15 years in the UK military before transferring to the Royal Australian Navy in 2019.
- His skiing accident in 2022 led to significant life changes.
