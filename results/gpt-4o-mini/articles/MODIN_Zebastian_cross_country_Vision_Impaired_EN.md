# Zebastian Modin: A Trailblazer in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Zebastian Modin is a prominent Paralympic athlete from Sweden, specializing in cross-country skiing. Competing in the B1 classification for vision-impaired athletes, he has become a formidable presence in Paralympic winter sports.

## Career Overview
Zebastian Modin’s career in cross-country skiing is marked by significant participation in various international competitions:

- **2010**: Debuted at the Vancouver Winter Paralympics, securing a bronze medal in the Men's 1 km Sprint.
- **2014**: Achieved success at the Sochi Winter Paralympics, winning gold in both the Men's 1 km Freestyle Sprint and the Men's 20 km Classical events.
- **2018**: Competed in the Pyeongchang Winter Paralympics, securing a silver medal in the Men's 1.5 km Sprint Classical and finishing 9th in the Men's 10 km and 11th in the Men's 20 km events.
- **2022**: Continued at the Beijing Winter Paralympics, winning a silver medal in the Men's 12.5 km Freestyle.
- **2025**: Won a gold medal at the 20 km Freestyle and secured first place in both the 10 km Individual Start and 10 km Mass Start in December.

## Major Achievements
- **Paralympic Medals**: 
  - Bronze Medal in the Men's 1 km Sprint at the 2010 Vancouver Paralympics.
  - Gold Medals in the Men's 1 km Freestyle Sprint and Men's 20 km Classical at the 2014 Sochi Paralympics.
  - Silver Medals in the Men's 1.5 km Sprint Classical at the 2018 Pyeongchang Paralympics and Men's 12.5 km Freestyle at the 2022 Beijing Paralympics.
  
- **World Championship Titles**:
  - Gold Medal at the 20 km Freestyle at the Trondheim Championships in 2025.
  - Titles in both 10 km Individual Start and 10 km Mass Start in December 2025.
