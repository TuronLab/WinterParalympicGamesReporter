# Scott Meenagh: A Trailblazer in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Scott Meenagh is a Scottish Paralympic athlete who competes in cross-country skiing. He represents Great Britain and competes in the LW12 disability classification.

## Career Overview
Scott Meenagh's career in adaptive sports began after he sustained life-altering injuries while serving in the British Army. Following his recovery, he transitioned into competitive sports, focusing on cross-country skiing and biathlon. His journey includes:

- **2018 Winter Paralympics**: Meenagh made his Paralympic debut, finishing 17th in the 15km free event.
- **2022 Winter Paralympics**: He improved his performance, achieving 12th place in the 18km classical event.
- **2023 World Para Nordic Skiing Championships**: Meenagh won a silver medal in the 12.5km individual biathlon event.

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing**:
  - 17th place in the 15km free event at the **2018 Winter Paralympics**.
  - 12th place in the 18km classical event at the **2022 Winter Paralympics**.
  - First British Nordic skier at a Paralympics in 20 years (2018).
  - Part of the first British relay team to finish the 4 × 2.5km relay event at a Paralympics (2022).
  
- **Biathlon**:
  - Silver medal in the 12.5km individual biathlon event at the **2023 World Para Nordic Skiing Championships**.
