# Kathrin Marchand: A Rising Star in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Kathrin Marchand is a German athlete competing in cross-country skiing, classified under the LW9 category (Standing). She is set to make her Paralympic debut in 2026, transitioning from a successful career in rowing to winter sports. Marchand suffered a stroke in 2021, which led to a reevaluation of her life and career, ultimately returning to sports as a form of rehabilitation.

## Career Overview
Kathrin Marchand's athletic career began in rowing, where she achieved significant success before shifting her focus to cross-country skiing. Her rowing career included participation in the Olympics until 2016. After her stroke, she returned to sports, this time in cross-country skiing.

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing:**
  - 2nd place in Mass Start at Szklarska Poreba-Jakuszyce (01-02-2026)

  - 9th place in Sprint Qualification at Szklarska Poreba-Jakuszyce (28-01-2026)
  - 9th place in Sprint Final at Szklarska Poreba-Jakuszyce (28-01-2026)
