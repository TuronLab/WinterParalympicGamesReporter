# YANG Qianru: A Rising Star in Paralympic Winter Sports

## Introduction  
Yang Qianru is a visually impaired cross-country skier from Beijing, China. Competing in the visually impaired (VI) classification, she participated in the 2022 Beijing Winter Paralympic Games.

## Career Overview  
Yang Qianru's career in winter sports has primarily centered around cross-country skiing and biathlon. She participated in the 2022 Winter Paralympics, showcasing her capabilities in cross-country skiing as well as biathlon.

## Major Achievements  
- **Cross-Country Skiing:**
  - 5th Place, Women's Sprint VI at the 2022 Beijing Winter Paralympic Games (March 5, 2022)
  - 5th Place, Women's Middle Distance VI at the 2022 Beijing Winter Paralympic Games (March 8, 2022)
  - 6th Place, Women's Sprint Free VI Qualification at the 2022 Beijing Winter Paralympic Games (March 9, 2022)
  - 4th Place, Women's Sprint Free VI Semifinal at the 2022 Beijing Winter Paralympic Games (March 9, 2022)
  - 5th Place, Women's Individual VI Final Round at the 2022 Beijing Winter Paralympic Games (March 11, 2022)

- **Biathlon:**
  - 5th Place, Women's Biathlon Event at the 2022 Beijing Winter Paralympic Games

## Paralympic Participations  
- **2022 Beijing Winter Paralympic Games**  
  - Cross-Country Skiing: Multiple top-6 finishes in Women’s Sprint VI, Women’s Middle Distance VI, and Women’s Individual VI events.
  - Biathlon: 5th Place finish in the Women’s Biathlon Event.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach  
Yang's training regimen includes extensive hours dedicated to skiing and biathlon shooting practices. She trains at a national snow sports training base in Baiyin City, which provides specialized facilities designed for athletes with disabilities. As a visually impaired competitor, she requires a guide for her training.
