# YU Shuang: A Rising Star in Para-Nordic Skiing

## Introduction  
Yu Shuang is a para-Nordic athlete from China, specializing in cross-country skiing and para-biathlon. Born on August 29, 2003, and classified as NS3 (Visually Impaired; B2 Classification), Yu made his Paralympic debut at the 2022 Winter Paralympics in Beijing.

## Career Overview  
Yu transitioned from biathlon to cross-country skiing. Post-2022, he has actively participated in international para-Nordic competitions.

### Sports Participated  
- **Cross-Country Skiing**
- **Para-Biathlon**

## Major Achievements  
- **Cross-Country Skiing:**
  - Gold medal in the interval start event at the 2024 World Cup.
- **Para-Biathlon:**
  - Bronze medal in Men's 12.5 km Individual Visually Impaired Biathlon at the 2022 Winter Paralympics.
  - Silver medal in Men's 7.5 km Sprint VI at the 2024 Para Biathlon World Cup in Vuokatti, Finland.
  - Bronze medal in Men's Sprint Pursuit VI at the 2024 Para Biathlon World Cup in Vuokatti, Finland.

## Performance Trends  
- Transitioned from biathlon to cross-country skiing.
- Consistently active in international para-Nordic competitions post-2022.
- In January 2024, secured 1st place in an interval start event in Lygna, Norway, and 2nd place in another event (FIS points of 132.08).

## Paralympic Participations  
- **2022 Winter Paralympics:**
  - **Sport:** Para-Biathlon
    - Result: Bronze medal in Men's 12.5 km Individual Visually Impaired Biathlon.
  - **Sport:** Cross-Country Skiing
    - Finished 4th in the Sprint Free VI semifinals.
    - Achieved 4th place in the Open 4x2.5 km Relay.

## Other Sports  
### Para-Biathlon Achievements  
- Bronze medal at the Europa Cup in Vuokatti, Finland in December 2021.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach  
Yu adopts a comprehensive training regimen that includes:
- High-altitude training facilities.
- Focus on skiing technique and endurance.
- Synchronization with his guide, Wang Guanyu.
- Use of audio cues for shooting accuracy.

## Notable Facts  
- Yu was born with congenital visual impairment.
- He grew up in rural Sichuan, China.
