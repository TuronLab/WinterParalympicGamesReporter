# Emma Archibald: A Rising Star in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Emma Archibald is a Canadian Paralympic athlete specializing in cross-country skiing. Competing in the standing category, Archibald has made significant strides in her sport. As she prepares for her upcoming participation in the Milano Cortina 2026 Paralympic Winter Games, Archibald's journey is one of resilience and determination.

## Career Overview
Emma Archibald's career in cross-country skiing has been marked by notable achievements. Born on April 16, 2003, in Mississauga, Ontario, she currently resides in Fall River, Nova Scotia. Archibald was born with Amniotic Band Syndrome and clubbed feet, challenges she has overcome.

- **2023**: Achieved double gold medals at the Canada Games. [Source: nordiqcanada.ca](https://nordiqcanada.ca/athlete/emma-archibald/)
- **2025**: Competed at the Para Cross-Country World Championships, finishing 9th in the women's standing 20 km and 13th in the 10 km event. [Source: uottawa.ca](https://www.uottawa.ca/en/news-all/nursing-student-emma-archibald-skis-canada-paralympics)
