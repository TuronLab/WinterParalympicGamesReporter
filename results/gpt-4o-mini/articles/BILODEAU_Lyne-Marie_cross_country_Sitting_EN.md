# BILODEAU Lyne-Marie: A Rising Star in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
BILODEAU Lyne-Marie is a Canadian cross-country skier competing in the LW12 classification. Born on April 5, 2001, in Sherbrooke, Quebec, she has emerged as a promising athlete in the realm of Paralympic winter sports. 

## Career Overview
Lyne-Marie's journey in cross-country skiing began by following her older brother into the sport. She attended her first Para-Nordic ski camp with the national team, which motivated her to join Team Canada. 

## Major Achievements
- **2022 Para-Nordic World Cup, Vuokatti, Finland**
  - 3rd place in 10km (Cross-Country)
  - 3rd place in 5km (Cross-Country)
- **2019 Canada Games, Red Deer, Alberta**
  - 1st place in multiple events (Cross-Country)

## Performance Trends
- Steady progression noted since joining the national team.
- Competed in multiple international events with notable placements.

## Paralympic Participations
- **Beijing 2022 Paralympic Winter Games**
  - Women's Long Distance Sitting: 9th place (2022-03-06)
  - Women's Sprint Sitting Qualification: 18th place (2022-03-09)
  - Women's Middle Distance Sitting: 13th place (2022-03-12)

## Other Sports

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Lyne-Marie's training approach is characterized by her early involvement in the sport, which was motivated by her brother. She trains under the guidance of coach Brian McKeever.

## Notable Facts
- Lyne-Marie was the youngest member of Canada’s Para Nordic team at the Beijing 2022 Paralympic Winter Games.
- She is affiliated with the Mauriski club and has personal sponsors, including Mazda Granby and Velo Cafe Endurance Aventure.
