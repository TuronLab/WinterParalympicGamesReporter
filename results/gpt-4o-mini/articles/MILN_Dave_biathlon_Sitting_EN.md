# The Journey of Dave Miln: A Paralympic Biathlon Athlete

## Introduction
David Miln, born on December 6, 1985, is an Australian athlete specializing in biathlon, competing in the LW12 classification as a sit skier. Miln suffered severe injuries in a skiing accident in 2022, resulting in amputations.

## Career Overview
- **2024**: Miln first competed for Australia in Para cross-country and Para biathlon in Canada.
- **2024/25 Season**: He participated in FIS international events.
- **2025**: Achieved recognition by winning a silver medal in advanced biathlon at the Invictus Games in Vancouver Whistler.

## Major Achievements
- **Biathlon**:
  - Silver medal in advanced biathlon at the 2025 Invictus Games.
  - Flag bearer for Australia at the Invictus Games 2025.

- **Other Sports**:
  - At the Invictus Games 2025, won 4 gold medals in swimming (50m free, 100m free, 50m breaststroke, 50m backstroke) and a silver in the mixed relay.

## Performance Trends
- Competed in FIS international events since the 2024/25 season.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2026 Winter Paralympics**: Selected to compete in biathlon and cross-country skiing in Milano Cortina.

## Other Sports
- **Wheelchair Basketball**: Competes for a club in Manly.
- **Downhill Skiing**: Competed for the British Navy team prior to his accident.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Miln emphasizes a military mindset in his training, focusing on perseverance through adversity.

## Notable Facts
- Miln is a former Royal Marine Commando and Mine Warfare and Clearance Diving Officer.
- His transition to adaptive sports came after a skiing accident in 2022, which led to his amputations.
