# Ekaterina Rumyantseva: A Paralympic Champion in Nordic Skiing

## Introduction
Ekaterina Rumyantseva, born on January 28, 1991, is a Russian athlete specializing in Paralympic Nordic skiing, specifically in cross-country skiing and biathlon. Competing in the LW5/7 disability classification, Rumyantseva participated in the 2018 Winter Paralympics.

## Career Overview
Ekaterina Rumyantseva's career in Paralympic sports has been marked by her performances in cross-country skiing and biathlon. She primarily competed in the 2018 Winter Paralympics.

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing:**
  - Gold Medal in Women's 6 km - Standing (2018 Winter Paralympics)
  - Gold Medal in Women's 10 km - Standing (2018 Winter Paralympics)
  - Gold Medal in Women's 15 km Free - Standing (2018 Winter Paralympics)
  - Silver Medal in Women's 7.5 km Classic - Standing (2018 Winter Paralympics)
  - Silver Medal in Women's 12.5 km - Standing (2018 Winter Paralympics)
  - 7th in Women's Sprint Classic 1.5 km - Standing (2018 Winter Paralympics)
  - 11th in Open Relay 4x2.5 km (2018 Winter Paralympics)

- **Biathlon:**
  - Gold Medal in Women's 6 km - Standing (2018 Winter Paralympics)
  - Silver Medal in Women's 12.5 km - Standing (2018 Winter Paralympics)
  - Silver Medal in Women's 7.5 km Classic - Standing (2018 Winter Paralympics)

- **Titles:**
  - Named "Disabled Female Athlete of the Year" in 2018 by the Ministry of Sport of Russia.
  - Awarded the International Sports Prize World Athlete of the Year in 2018.
