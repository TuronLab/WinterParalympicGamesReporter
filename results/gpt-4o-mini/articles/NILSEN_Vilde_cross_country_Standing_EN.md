# Vilde Nilsen: A Rising Star in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Vilde Nilsen is a Norwegian Paralympic athlete specializing in cross-country skiing. Competing in the LW4 classification, Nilsen has made significant strides in her sport. Her participation in the Winter Paralympics and other major competitions has established her as a competitor in the world of adaptive sports.

## Career Overview
Vilde Nilsen's career in cross-country skiing began to gain prominence with her participation in the 2018 Winter Paralympics. Since then, she has consistently competed at high levels, including the World Para Snow Sports Championships and various World Cup events. Nilsen also participates in biathlon.

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing:**
  - Silver medal at the 2018 Winter Paralympics in Women's 1.5km Sprint Classic Standing.
  - Gold medal at the 2021 World Para Snow Sports Championships in Women's 10km Standing.
  - Silver medal at the 2022 Winter Paralympics in Women's Sprint Free Standing.
  - Bronze medal at the 2022 Winter Paralympics in Open 4x2.5km Relay.
  
- **Biathlon:**
  - Silver medal in Biathlon at World Cups.
  - Bronze medal in Biathlon at the 2019 World Para Nordic Skiing Championships.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2018 Winter Paralympics:**
  - Sport: Cross-Country Skiing
  - Event: Women's 1.5km Sprint Classic Standing
  - Result: Silver Medal

- **2022 Winter Paralympics:**
  - Sport: Cross-Country Skiing
  - Event: Women's Sprint Free Standing
  - Result: Silver Medal
  - Event: Open 4x2.5km Relay
  - Result: Bronze Medal

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Vilde Nilsen dedicates approximately 700 hours annually to her training regimen, focusing on both physical and mental development.

## Notable Facts
- Born on January 12, 2001, in Tromsø, Norway, Nilsen was diagnosed with linear scleroderma at age 8, which has affected her mobility.
