# Alexander Ehler: A Journey Through Paralympic Biathlon

## Introduction
Alexander Ehler, born on June 26, 1969, in Leninogorsk, Kazakhstan, is a German athlete specializing in biathlon and cross-country skiing. Competing in the LW4 classification, Ehler has made significant strides in the Paralympic sports arena, particularly since his debut at the 2018 Paralympics. He has a leg length difference of 9 cm due to a motorcycle accident.

## Career Overview
Ehler's sporting career began at the age of nine. His participation in the Paralympics commenced in 2018.

## Major Achievements
- **Paralympics 2018 (Pyeongchang):**
  - Bronze in Cross-Country Skiing 4x2.5 km Mixed Relay
  - 5th place in Biathlon 7.5 km
- **World Championships:**
  - 3x Bronze in Cross-Country Skiing 4x2.5 km Mixed Relay (Toblach 2025, Östersund 2023, Prince George 2019)
  - 4th place in Biathlon 12.5 km (Prince George 2024)
- **World Cup:**
  - 2nd place in Biathlon 15 km (Östersund 2019)

## Performance Trends
- Recent results:
  - 5th place in Biathlon 7.5 km
  - 4th place in Biathlon 12.5 km
  - 2nd place in Biathlon 15 km

## Paralympic Participations
- **2018 Paralympics (Pyeongchang):**
  - Sport: Cross-Country Skiing
  - Result: Bronze in 4x2.5 km Mixed Relay
  - Result: 5th place in Biathlon 7.5 km

## Other Sports
- **Cross-Country Skiing:**
  - Bronze in Cross-Country Skiing 4x2.5 km Mixed Relay at the Paralympics 2018

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Ehler's training has been impacted by a COVID-19 infection prior to the 2022 Paralympics. He emphasizes a calm approach to competitions and relies on team support for motivation.

## Notable Facts
Ehler has two daughters who are successful fencers.
