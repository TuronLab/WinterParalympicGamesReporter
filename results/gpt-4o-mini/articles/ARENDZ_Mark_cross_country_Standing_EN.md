# Mark Arendz: A Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing Champion

## Introduction
Mark Arendz is a Canadian athlete specializing in cross-country skiing and biathlon. He competes in the LW6 classification, which is designated for standing athletes.

## Career Overview
Mark Arendz's career in Paralympic sports began with his participation in the 2010 Vancouver Paralympic Games. His career trajectory includes:

- **2010 Vancouver Paralympic Games**: 12th (10km C), 7th (5km F), 9th (Sprint C).
- **2014 Sochi Paralympic Games**: 11th (10km Standing), 11th (12.5km Standing).
- **2017 World Para-Nordic Ski Championships**: 3rd (Cross Country Middle Free), 5th (Cross Country Long).
- **2018 PyeongChang Paralympic Games**: 3rd (1.5km Sprint Classic), 3rd (10km Classic), 2nd (4x2.5km Mixed Relay).
- **2019 Prince George World Para-Nordic Ski Championships**: 2nd (Middle Distance), 3rd (Sprint), 2nd (Long Distance).
- **2022 Beijing Paralympic Games**: 1st (Middle Distance), 3rd (Sprint), 3rd (Mixed Relay).

## Major Achievements
Mark Arendz has an impressive list of achievements in cross-country skiing and biathlon, including:

- **2018 PyeongChang Paralympic Games**:
  - Gold Medal: 15km Standing
  - Silver Medals: 7.5km Standing, 12.5km Standing
  - Bronze Medals: 1.5km Sprint Classic, 10km Classic, Mixed Relay
- **2017 World Para-Nordic Ski Championships**:
  - Gold Medal: Cross Country Middle Free
  - Bronze Medal: Cross Country Long
- **2019 Prince George World Para-Nordic Ski Championships**:
  - Silver Medal: Middle Distance
  - Bronze Medal: Sprint
  - Silver Medal: Long Distance
- **2022 Beijing Paralympic Games**:
  - Gold Medal: Middle Distance
  - Bronze Medals: Sprint, Mixed Relay

## Performance Trends
Mark Arendz has shown consistent improvement over the years, particularly highlighted by his performances in 2018 and 2022. At the 2018 Paralympics, he achieved medals in every event he entered.

## Paralympic Participations
Mark Arendz has participated in four Paralympic Games, with the following results:

- **2010 Vancouver Paralympic Games**:
  - 12th (10km C), 7th (5km F), 9th (Sprint C)
- **2014 Sochi Paralympic Games**:
  - 11th (10km Standing), 11th (12.5km Standing)
- **2018 PyeongChang Paralympic Games**:
  - 1st (15km Standing), 2nd (7.5km Standing), 2nd (12.5km Standing), 3rd (1.5km Sprint Classic), 3rd (10km Classic), 3rd (Mixed Relay)
- **2022 Beijing Paralympic Games**:
  - 1st (Middle Distance), 3rd (Sprint), 3rd (Mixed Relay)

## Other Sports

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Mark Arendz's training regimen focuses on both cross-country skiing and biathlon. He utilizes specialized equipment and techniques tailored to his classification.

## Notable Facts
- Mark Arendz was born on March 3, 1990, in Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island.
- He lost his left arm in a farming accident at the age of seven.
- Arendz is also active in community service, including efforts to build schools in Nicaragua.
