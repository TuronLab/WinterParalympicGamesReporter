# Ryohei Ariyasu: A Resilient Force in Paralympic Winter Sports

## Introduction
Ryohei Ariyasu is a dedicated para-athlete from Japan, born on February 2, 1987. He competes primarily in cross-country skiing and falls under the NS3 category for vision impairment. His participation in the Paralympic Games highlights his commitment to excelling in adaptive sports across multiple disciplines, including rowing.

## Career Overview
Ryohei Ariyasu's athletic journey includes competing in rowing events leading up to the **Tokyo 2020 Paralympics** and debuting in cross-country skiing during the **Beijing 2022 Paralympic Winter Games**.

## Major Achievements
Ryohei Ariyasu's significant achievements throughout his athletic career include:

### Cross-Country Skiing
- **Beijing 2022 Paralympics**:
  - Men's Long Distance Classic VI: 7th place (March 7, 2022)
  - Men's Sprint Free VI: 16th place (Qualification, March 9, 2022)
  - Men's Middle Distance Free VI: 16th place (March 12, 2022)

### Rowing
- **Tokyo 2020 Paralympics**:  
  - Competed in the PR3 Mixed Coxed Four with placements of:
    - 6th in Heat 2
    - Participation in Final B.
    
- **Rowing Competitions**:
  - 2021: PR3 Men's Pair (Final) at various world events with placements around 5th to 6th.

## Paralympic Participations
- **Tokyo 2020 Paralympics** (Rowing):
  - Event: PR3 Mixed Coxed Four; Result: 6th in Heat 2 and Final B.

- **Beijing 2022 Paralympics** (Cross-Country Skiing):
  - Men's Long Distance Classic VI: 7th place
  - Men's Sprint Free VI: 16th place (Qualification)
  - Men's Middle Distance Free VI: 16th place

## Other Sports
