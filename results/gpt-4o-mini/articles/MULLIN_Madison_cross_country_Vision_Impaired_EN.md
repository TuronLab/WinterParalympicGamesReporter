# Madison Mullin: Rising Star in Para Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Madison Mullin is a Canadian para athlete competing in the sport of Para Cross-Country Skiing. She is classified in the NS3 category, which pertains to visually impaired skiers. 

## Career Overview
Madison Mullin launched her sporting career in alpine skiing but has since transitioned to cross-country skiing. Over time, she has showcased her talent at various competitions, notably achieving podium finishes in major events including the Canada Winter Games.

## Major Achievements
- **2023 Canada Winter Games**: 
  - Gold in 1.2km sprint
  - Silver in 2.5km classic
  - Silver in 5km freestyle
- Earned a spot on the National Para-Nordic Prospects team.
- Multiple podium finishes in Continental Cup races.

## Performance Trends
- Demonstrated notable national and international success, particularly with podium finishes at the Canada Winter Games.
- Consistent improvement noted throughout her participation in the World Cup circuit.

## Paralympic Participations
- Currently preparing for the **2026 Paralympic Games** in Para Cross-Country Skiing.

## Other Sports
- **Alpine Skiing**: 
  - Competed at a high level prior to transitioning to cross-country skiing.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
- Madison trains under head coach Brian McKeever, with racing support from her guide, currently Tory Audet (previously Faith Goudie).
- Participated in the elite Equinox International Junior training camp in Norway.

## Notable Facts
- Born on March 16, 2007, in Fergus, Ontario.
- Diagnosed with congenital glaucoma, which has led to her vision impairment.
