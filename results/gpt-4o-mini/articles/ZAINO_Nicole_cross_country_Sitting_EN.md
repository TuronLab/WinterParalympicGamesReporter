# Nicole Zaino: A Rising Star in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Nicole Zaino is a cross-country skier representing the United States of America. Born on February 25, 1996, she competes in the LW11.5 classification for sitting athletes. Zaino is currently active in her sport and is preparing for her participation in the Paralympic Winter Games Milano Cortina 2026.

## Career Overview
Nicole Zaino's career in cross-country skiing began with her first para Nordic skiing race at the U.S. Paralympics Nordic Skiing Sit Ski Nationals in January 2023. Since then, she has participated in various competitions and achieved notable results. Zaino has also played sled hockey for the Seattle Kraken from 2019 to 2023 and has engaged in recreational alpine 4-track skiing.

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing:**
  - 4th in middle-distance biathlon (2024)
  - 6th in middle-distance cross-country race (2024)
  - 5th in cross-country sprint (2024)
  - 6th in 12.5km Individual - Sitting - Women (2025)
  - 8th in 7.5km Sprint - Sitting - Women (2025)
  - 6th in Sprint Pursuit - Sitting - Women (2025)
  - Qualified for the U.S. team for the Paralympic Winter Games Milano Cortina 2026
  - Competed at the 2025 IBU Para Biathlon World Championships

## Performance Trends
- Steady improvement noted throughout the 2024-25 season.
- Qualified for the World Cup season finale in Prince George, British Columbia (2024).
- Consistent top 10 finishes in various events.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2026 Paralympic Winter Games (Milano Cortina)**: Qualified for the U.S. team.

## Other Sports
- **Sled Hockey:**
  - Played for Seattle Kraken from 2019-2023.
  
- **Recreational Alpine 4-track skiing:** Engaged in this sport.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Nicole Zaino trains year-round with a focus on poling technique, climbing, and endurance. Her training regimen includes the use of roller skis and mountainboards for conditioning. She applies principles of biomechanics to improve her efficiency in skiing.

## Notable Facts
- Hometown: Brookfield, Connecticut.
- Education: Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering from Clarkson University; PhD in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Washington (2023).
- Background: Suffered a stroke at age 8, resulting in paralysis on the left side of her body.
