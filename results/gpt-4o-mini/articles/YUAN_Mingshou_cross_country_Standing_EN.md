# YUAN Mingshou: A Rising Star in Paralympic Cross-Country

## Introduction
YUAN Mingshou is a prominent Chinese athlete specializing in cross-country skiing and para-biathlon. Born on July 6, 1996, he competes in the NS3 classification for visually impaired athletes. His participation in the 2022 Winter Paralympics marked a significant milestone in his sporting career.

## Career Overview
YUAN Mingshou's career in para-sports has been characterized by notable achievements. He primarily competes in para-biathlon, which combines cross-country skiing and rifle shooting. His journey began with local competitions, leading to his debut at the 2022 Winter Paralympics in Beijing, where he participated in multiple events.

## Major Achievements
- **2022 Winter Paralympics, Beijing**:
  - Bronze medal in Men's 12.5 km individual visually impaired biathlon.
  - 4th place in Men's sprint biathlon.
  - 6th place in Men's middle-distance biathlon.
  - 4th place in Sprint cross-country semifinal.
  - 4th place in Open 4x2.5 km relay.
- **2024 World Cup**:
  - Gold medal in the interval start event.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2022 Winter Paralympics, Beijing**:
  - Sport: Para-biathlon
    - Men's 12.5 km individual visually impaired biathlon - Bronze medal.
    - Men's sprint biathlon - 4th place.
    - Men's middle-distance biathlon - 6th place.
    - Sprint cross-country - 4th in semifinal.
    - Open 4x2.5 km relay - 4th place.

## Other Sports
- **Para-biathlon**:
  - Bronze medal in the men's 12.5 km visually impaired biathlon at the 2022 Winter Paralympics.
  - Silver and bronze medals in various events at the 2024 Para Biathlon World Cup.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
YUAN Mingshou employs a rigorous training regimen, focusing on high-altitude training facilities to enhance his skiing technique and endurance. His preparation includes:
- Synchronization with his guide, Wang Guanyu, to optimize performance during skiing and shooting.
- Utilization of audio cues to improve shooting accuracy.

## Notable Facts
- YUAN was born with a congenital visual impairment and is classified under the B2 category for visually impaired athletes.
- He grew up in Qu County, Sichuan Province, China, in a supportive family environment, overcoming significant challenges related to his visual impairment.
