### Leonie Walter: A Rising Star in Para Biathlon

#### Introduction
Leonie Maria Walter, born on January 17, 2004, hails from Germany and competes in Para Biathlon and Para Cross-Country Skiing. She is classified in the visually impaired B2 category due to her condition of macular hypoplasia. Walter captured attention during her participation in the 2022 Winter Paralympics.

#### Career Overview
Leonie Walter’s athletic journey in biathlon began to gain international prominence with her participation in the 2022 Winter Paralympics in Beijing. Competing in the visually impaired category, she has also participated in Para Cross-Country Skiing events.

#### Major Achievements
- **Winter Paralympics 2022, Beijing**
  - Gold Medal: 10 km visually impaired
  - Bronze Medal: 6 km visually impaired
  - Bronze Medal: 12.5 km visually impaired
- **2025 FISU World University Games**
  - Gold Medal: 10 km freestyle
- **Biathlon World Cup 2025, Val di Fiemme**
  - 1st place: 7.5 km sprint

#### Performance Trends
- The **2022 Beijing Winter Paralympics** marked Walter's debut at a major international event, where she won three medals.
- Notable performance at the **2025 Biathlon World Cup** in Val di Fiemme, where she achieved a first-place finish in the 7.5 km sprint.

#### Paralympic Participations
- **2022 Winter Paralympics, Beijing**
  - 10 km visually impaired: Gold Medal
  - 6 km visually impaired: Bronze Medal
  - 12.5 km visually impaired: Bronze Medal
- Planned Participation:
  - **2026 Milano Cortina Winter Paralympics**

#### Other Sports
Leonie Walter also competes in Para Cross-Country Skiing with the following achievements:
- **Winter Paralympics 2022, Beijing**
  - Bronze Medal: 15 km classical
- **2023 World Championships in Cross-Country Skiing**
  - Participated in 18 km individual and a relay event.

#### Preparation Style / Training Approach
Walter's coaching is led by Ralf Rombach. Specific details of her training regimes are not publicly detailed.

#### Notable Facts
