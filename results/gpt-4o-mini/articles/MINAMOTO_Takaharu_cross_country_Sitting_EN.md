# Takaharu Minamoto: A Rising Star in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Takaharu Minamoto is a 45-year-old male athlete from Japan, specializing in cross-country skiing within the Para Nordic category. He competes in the LW12 classification, which is designated for athletes who are sitting. Minamoto is currently active in his sport and is affiliated with Amgen KK. He resides in Kitahiroshima, Japan, and is preparing for the upcoming 2026 Milan-Cortina Paralympics.

## Career Overview
Takaharu Minamoto's career in cross-country skiing has seen a steady progression, marked by participation in both national and international competitions. His journey includes:

- **2025**: Achieved notable placements in various events, including the All Japan Cross-Country Ski Championships and the FIS Paracontinental Cup.
- **2025**: Also participated in biathlon events.

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing**:
  - 3rd place in the 1km (sitting) at the 27th All Japan Cross-Country Ski Championships for the Disabled (2025).
  - 3rd place in the 2.5km LW12 at the 27th All Japan Para Cross-Country Skiing Championships (2025).
  - 2nd place in the 5km (sitting) at the FIS Paracontinental Cup (Asia Cup) in Sapporo (2025).
  - 7th place in the Sprint Combined LW12 at the FIS Paracontinental Cup (Asia Cup) in Sapporo (2025).
  
- **Biathlon**:
  - 13th place in Sprint 7.5km (sitting) at the 2025 IBU Para-Biathlon World Championships.
  - 11th place in Sprint 7.5km (sitting) at the 2025 IBU Para Biathlon World Cup.
