# Ivan Golubkov: A Rising Star in Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Ivan Golubkov is a prominent Russian athlete specializing in cross-country skiing. Born on December 4, 1995, he competes in the LW11.5 classification, which is designated for athletes with physical impairments who compete in a sitting position. Golubkov is set to make his Paralympic debut at the Milano Cortina 2026 Games.

## Career Overview
Golubkov's career in cross-country skiing has been marked by notable achievements. He has also competed in biathlon. His competitive journey includes participation in various international events.

## Major Achievements
- **Six-time World Champion** in Cross-Country Skiing and Biathlon.
- **World Cup Champion** at age 18.
- **Overall Crystal Globe Winner** by age 20.
- **Lillehammer 2021 World Para Snow Sports Championships**:
  - 1st place in Men's Middle Distance - Classic Sitting (January 13, 2022)
  - 1st place in Men's Sprint Sitting (January 15, 2022)
  - 1st place in Men's Middle Distance Sitting (January 16, 2022)
  - 1st place in Men's Long Distance - Free Style Sitting (January 18, 2022)
  - 6th place in Men's Long Distance Sitting (January 20, 2022)
  - 1st place in Men's Sprint - Classic Sitting (January 22, 2022)
  - 1st place in Open Relay (January 23, 2022)

## Performance Trends
- Golubkov has exhibited a consistent upward trend in performance, highlighted by multiple gold medal wins in major competitions.

## Paralympic Participations
- **Milano Cortina 2026**: Scheduled to compete in cross-country skiing (debut).

## Other Sports
- **Biathlon**: Competes alongside cross-country skiing, although specific achievements in this sport are not publicly available.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Golubkov emphasizes home training as a crucial component of his preparation.

## Notable Facts
- Ivan Golubkov hails from Inta in the Komi Republic, Russia, and represents the Moscow Academy of Cross-Country Skiing and Biathlon.
