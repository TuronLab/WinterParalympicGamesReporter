# Temuri Dadiani: A Journey of Resilience in Paralympic Cross Country Skiing

## Introduction
Temuri Dadiani is a Georgian athlete specializing in cross country skiing, competing in the LW12 classification for sitting athletes. Dadiani lost both legs due to a landmine explosion while serving in Afghanistan in 2011. Dadiani began competing in para-nordic skiing in 2017 and has since represented Georgia in multiple international competitions, including the Paralympic Winter Games.

## Career Overview
- **2017**: Began competing in para-nordic skiing.
- **2018**: Competed in the Pyeongchang Paralympic Winter Games.
- **2021**: Participated in the Lillehammer World Para Snow Sports Championships.
- **2022**: Competed in the Beijing Paralympic Winter Games.
- **2023**: Achieved a gold medal in the Para Powerlifting World Cup.
