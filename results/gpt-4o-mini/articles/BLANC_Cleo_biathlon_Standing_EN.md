# Cléo Blanc: Rising Star in Para Biathlon

## Introduction
Cléo Blanc is a French para biathlete, competing in the LW9 classification. Born in Bourg-Saint-Maurice, Savoie, she has made strides in her sporting career since her debut in 2020. Diagnosed with hemiparesis due to a stroke at birth, which affects the left side of her body, Cléo has shown resilience in her pursuit of excellence in para sports.

## Career Overview
Cléo began her journey in para sports by practicing biathlon and ski de fond (cross-country skiing) in 2018. She transitioned to competitive events in 2020. Her career has been marked by achievements in both biathlon and cross-country skiing.

## Major Achievements
- **Biathlon:**
  - 2024: Champion of France in para biathlon
  - 2025: Champion of France in para biathlon, all categories combined
  - 2025: Winner of the general classification of the Coupe de France
  - 2025: Winner of the young potential trophy

- **Cross-Country Skiing (Ski de Fond):**
  - 2025: Vice-champion of France in para ski de fond (classic)
  - 2024: Competed in the 10km at the Continental Cup Gålå (skating)

## Performance Trends
- Achieved a time of 26'49''2 in the 2024 Championship, with an adjusted time of 23'52''1 after applying the handicap coefficient.

## Paralympic Participations
- Currently aiming for qualification for the **2030 Paralympic Games**.

## Other Sports
- **Cross-Country Skiing (Ski de Fond):**
  - 2025: Vice-champion of France in para ski de fond (classic)
  - 2024: Competed in the 10km at the Continental Cup Gålå (skating)

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Cléo is integrated into the Pôle France ski nordique at Lycée Reinach, balancing academic commitments with training.

## Notable Facts
- Cléo started her journey in biathlon and ski de fond in 2018 and has been competing since 2020.
- Her diagnosis of hemiparesis has not deterred her from achieving success in her sports.
