# Natalie Wilkie: A Rising Star in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Natalie Wilkie is a Canadian Paralympic athlete specializing in cross-country skiing. Born on January 21, 2001, in Salmon Arm, British Columbia, she competes in the LW8 classification, which is designated for women in the standing category. 

## Career Overview
Natalie Wilkie's career in cross-country skiing began following a life-altering accident in 2016, where she lost four fingers in a woodworking incident. This event led her to pursue para cross-country skiing.

- **2018 PyeongChang Paralympics**: Wilkie made her Paralympic debut, showcasing her talent with multiple podium finishes.
- **2019 World Para Nordic Skiing Championships**: Continued to build on her success with strong performances.
- **2022 Beijing Paralympics**: Further solidified her status as a top athlete in her category with additional medals.

## Major Achievements
- **2018 PyeongChang Paralympics**:
  - Gold: Women's 7.5 km Classic Standing
  - Silver: 4x2.5 km Mixed Relay
  - Bronze: Women's 1.5 km Sprint Classic Standing
  - 6th: Women's 15 km Free Standing
- **2019 World Para Nordic Skiing Championships**:
  - Silver: Women's Long C, Standing
  - Silver: Mixed Relay
  - 4th: Women's Sprint F, Standing
  - 4th: Women's Middle F, Standing
- **2022 Beijing Paralympics**:
  - Gold: Women's 10 km Free Standing
  - Silver: Women's Middle Distance Free Standing
  - Bronze: Mixed 4x2.5 km Relay
  - 15th: Women's Sprint Standing
- **Titles**:
  - Canadian Paralympic Sport Award for Best Paralympic Debut by a Female Athlete (2018).

## Performance Trends
- Emerged as a top competitor at the **2018 Paralympics**, winning three medals.
- Maintained a strong presence on the podium in subsequent championships, including the **2019 World Para Nordic Skiing Championships** and the **2022 Beijing Paralympics**.
