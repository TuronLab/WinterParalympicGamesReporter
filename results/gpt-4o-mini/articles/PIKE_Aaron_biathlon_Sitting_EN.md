# Aaron Pike: A Profile of Resilience and Achievement in Paralympic Sports

## Introduction
Aaron Pike is an athlete from the United States of America, competing in the sport of biathlon. He is classified in the LW10 category, which is designated for sitting athletes. Pike has participated in multiple Paralympic events across both winter and summer disciplines.

## Career Overview
Aaron Pike's athletic career includes participation in both biathlon and cross-country skiing, as well as wheelchair racing. He began his journey after sustaining a spinal injury at the age of 13. Pike is a six-time Paralympian.

- **2012 Summer Paralympics**: Competed in wheelchair racing.
- **2014 Winter Paralympics**: Made his debut in biathlon.
- **2016 Summer Paralympics**: Continued to compete in athletics.
- **2018 Winter Paralympics**: Further established his presence in biathlon.
- **2020 Summer Paralympics**: Participated again in athletics.
- **2022 Winter Paralympics**: Competed in biathlon.
- **Upcoming 2026 Winter Paralympics**: Set to compete in Milan Cortina.

## Major Achievements
- **2014 Paralympic Winter Games**:
  - Men's 7.5 km Sitting: 19th
  - Men's 15 km Sitting: 12th
  - Men's 1 km Sprint Sitting: 14th (Qualification)
  - Men's 15 km Sitting: 19th
  - Men's 10 km Sitting: 14th
- **Six-time Paralympian** across both summer and winter events.

## Paralympic Participations

## Other Sports
- **Wheelchair Racing**:
  - Competed in the 2012, 2016, and 2020 Summer Paralympics.
- **Cross-Country Skiing**:
  - Achieved significant results alongside his biathlon performances.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Aaron Pike engages in year-round training that encompasses both biathlon and athletics. His preparation includes cross-training to enhance performance across disciplines. He emphasizes teamwork and shared experiences with fellow athletes.

## Notable Facts
- Pike is known for his resilience and determination, having turned to adaptive sports following a spinal injury.
- He is engaged to fellow athlete Oksana Masters.
