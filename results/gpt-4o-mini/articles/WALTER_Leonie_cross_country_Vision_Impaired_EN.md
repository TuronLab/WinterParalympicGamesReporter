# Leonie Maria Walter: Rising Star in Paralympic Winter Sports

## Introduction
Leonie Maria Walter is a 22-year-old German athlete specializing in cross-country skiing and biathlon. Competing in the B2 classification for visually impaired athletes due to her condition of macular hypoplasia, Walter has made significant strides in her sports, showcasing her talents on the international stage.

## Career Overview
Leonie Walter began her competitive career in winter sports, participating primarily in cross-country skiing and biathlon events. Through the years, she has demonstrated her remarkable abilities, quickly climbing the ranks within her categories. 

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing:**
  - **2022 Winter Paralympics (Beijing):**
    - Bronze medal in 15 km classical cross-country skiing.
  - **2023 World Championships (Östersund):**
    - Gold in 18 km individual.
    - Gold in 10 km freestyle.
    - Gold in 4x2.5 km mixed relay.
  
- **Biathlon:**
  - **2022 Winter Paralympics (Beijing):**
    - Gold medal in women's 10 km visually impaired.
    - Bronze medals in women's 6 km and 12.5 km visually impaired.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2022 Winter Paralympics (Beijing):**
  - Cross-Country Skiing, Bronze in 15 km classical.
  - Biathlon, Gold in women's 10 km visually impaired; Bronze in women's 6 km and 12.5 km visually impaired.

## Other Sports
- **Biathlon:**
  - Competed at the 2022 Winter Paralympics, achieving notable success.

## Achievements in Other Sports
- **2022 Winter Paralympics (Biathlon):**
  - Gold medal in women's 10 km visually impaired.
  - Bronze medals in women's 6 km and 12.5 km visually impaired.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
- Walter's training regimen emphasizes endurance and technique refinement, specifically tailored for the disciplines of skiing and shooting in biathlon. Her preparation is further supported by her guide, Pirmin Strecker, who plays a pivotal role in ensuring her safe navigation and performance during competitive events.
