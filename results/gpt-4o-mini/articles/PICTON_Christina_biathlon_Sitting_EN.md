# Christina Picton: A Trailblazer in Paralympic Biathlon

## Introduction
Christina Picton is a Canadian athlete specializing in biathlon, competing in the Nordic Sit-Ski category. Born on April 6, 1993, she has made significant strides in the world of Paralympic sports, particularly in biathlon. Picton's journey in sports is marked by her resilience and determination, especially following her amputation.

## Career Overview
Christina Picton began her career in Nordic skiing and biathlon in 2018. She transitioned from ice sledge hockey, where she was a prominent player for 17 years, to focusing on biathlon. She served as captain of the Canada Women's National Ice Sledge Hockey Team during the 2014-2015 season.

## Major Achievements
- **2022 Paralympic Games**: Competed in Beijing, China, achieving top-eight finishes in all events participated.
- **2022/2023 Season**: Finished as the 2nd Overall Female Para Nordic Sit Skier.
- **Parabiathlon World Cup**: Best result of 4th place in the 12.5 km individual event (January 2026).
