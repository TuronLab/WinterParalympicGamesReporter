# David Chavez: Trailblazer in Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
David Chavez is a 27-year-old athlete from El Salvador, competing in cross-country skiing. He is classified in the LW10.5 category (sitting) and is currently preparing for his debut at the Milano Cortina 2026 Paralympic Winter Games. Chavez's participation marks a historic moment as he becomes the first athlete from El Salvador to compete in a Winter Games, whether Olympic or Paralympic.

## Career Overview
David Chavez's athletic career began after a spinal cord injury in 2015. Initially, he competed in para surfing before transitioning to cross-country skiing. His qualification for the upcoming Paralympic Games is noted.

## Major Achievements
- **Milano Cortina 2026 Paralympic Winter Games**: Qualified as the first athlete from El Salvador to compete in a Winter Games.

## Performance Trends
- David has shown improvement in his performance, particularly in the 10-kilometer event, where he has reduced his time to 38 minutes and reached 37th in the world rankings.

## Paralympic Participations
- **Milano Cortina 2026**: Competing in cross-country skiing.

## Other Sports
- **Para Surfing**: Competed at a high level before transitioning to cross-country skiing.

## Achievements in Other Sports

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
David trains between two to three hours daily, utilizing a combination of machine sessions and strength training on sand.

## Notable Facts
