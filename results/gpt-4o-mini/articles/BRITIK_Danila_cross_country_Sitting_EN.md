# Danila Britik: A Rising Star in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Danila Britik is a Russian athlete specializing in cross-country skiing and biathlon. Born on April 5, 1995, he competes in the LW10.5 classification, which is designated for athletes with a physical impairment who compete in a sitting position. Britik is currently an active competitor and represents the Russian Paralympic Committee, participating in the Milano Cortina 2026 Paralympic Winter Games.

## Career Overview
Danila Britik's career in winter sports has been marked by significant achievements, particularly in cross-country skiing and biathlon. His journey can be summarized as follows:

- **2022**: Britik made a notable impact at the Lillehammer World Para Nordic Skiing Championships, where he showcased his skills in both cross-country skiing and biathlon.

## Major Achievements
Danila Britik has accumulated several accolades throughout his career, particularly at the 2022 Lillehammer World Para Nordic Skiing Championships. His major achievements include:

- **Cross-Country Skiing**:
  - Bronze in Men's Middle Distance - Classic Sitting at the 2022 Lillehammer Championships.
  - Bronze in Men's Long Distance - Free Style Sitting at the 2022 Lillehammer Championships.
  - Bronze in Men's Long Distance Sitting at the 2022 Lillehammer Championships.
  - Bronze in Men's Sprint - Classic Sitting at the 2022 Lillehammer Championships.
  - 5 total bronze medals at the 2022 Lillehammer World Para Nordic Skiing Championships (3 in cross-country, 2 in biathlon).

- **Biathlon**:
  - Bronze in Men's 10 km at the 2022 Lillehammer World Para Nordic Skiing Championships.
  - Bronze in Men's 12.5 km at the 2022 Lillehammer World Para Nordic Skiing Championships.
