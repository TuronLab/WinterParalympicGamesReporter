# An Analytical Overview of Paralympic Athlete Lennart Volkert

## Introduction  
Lennart Volkert is an athlete from Germany, competing in the sport of biathlon. He participates in the LW10 classification, designated for athletes with vision impairments. 

## Career Overview  
Lennart Volkert transitioned into biathlon following his initial passion for football, where he served as a captain until vision impairment due to Zapfendystrophie necessitated a change in sports. He has built a career in Nordic skiing and biathlon.

## Major Achievements  
- **9th place** at a biathlon event in Finland, December 2022.
- **8th place** in subsequent competitions in Finland, with mentions of challenges such as damaged ski poles.
- **International German Para Cross-Country Skiing Champion** on his 22nd birthday, featuring a combined classification of men's events.

## Performance Trends  
- Steadily improving performance over the past few years. 

## Paralympic Participations  
- **Upcoming Participation:** Anticipated competitor in the next Paralympic Winter Games.

## Other Sports (if any)  
- **Football:** Initially pursued football and acted as team captain. Transitioned to Nordic skiing after vision impairment. 

## Preparation Style / Training Approach  
- Training alongside specialized coaches for visually impaired athletes, focusing on both skating and classical skiing techniques. 
- Receives training plans from state trainers associated with the developmental squad.

## Notable Facts  
- Originally from Markt Schwaben, Germany, where he attends Adolf-Weber-Gymnasium in Munich. Post-graduation, Volkert plans to relocate to Freiburg for training opportunities.
