# Collin Cameron: A Trailblazer in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Collin Cameron is a Canadian athlete specializing in cross-country skiing. He competes in the sit-skier category. Cameron's participation in the Paralympic Games has earned him multiple podium finishes.

## Career Overview
Cameron's journey in para sports began when he first tried Para Nordic skiing in November 2015, leading to his competitive debut in January 2016. He has participated in three Paralympic Games.

## Major Achievements
- **Paralympic Winter Games:**
  - 2022 Beijing, CHN:
    - 3rd place (15 km)
    - 3rd place (sprint)
    - 3rd place (mixed relay)
    - 4th place (10 km)
  - 2018 PyeongChang, KOR:
    - 3rd place (biathlon 7.5 km)
    - 3rd place (biathlon 15 km)
    - 3rd place (cross-country relay)
    - 4th place (sprint)
    - 5th place (15 km)
- **World Para Nordic Skiing Championships:**
  - 2019:
    - Gold medal (sprint)
    - 2 Silver medals (events not specified)

## Performance Trends
- Achieved three bronze medals in both the 2018 and 2022 Paralympic Games.
- Recorded five podium results in seven starts during the 2020 competitive season.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2018 PyeongChang, KOR:**
  - Biathlon 7.5 km: 3rd place
  - Biathlon 15 km: 3rd place
  - Cross-country relay: 3rd place
  - Sprint: 4th place
  - 15 km: 5th place
- **2022 Beijing, CHN:**
  - 15 km: 3rd place
  - Sprint: 3rd place
  - Mixed relay: 3rd place
  - 10 km: 4th place
- **Upcoming 2026 Paralympic Games:** Participation confirmed.

## Other Sports
- **Para Ice Hockey:**
  - Played for the Northern Sliders in Sudbury. 

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Cameron trains under coach Brian McKeever and wax technician Ian Daffern. 

## Notable Facts
- Born on March 31, 1988, in Barrie, Ontario, Cameron hails from Bracebridge, Ontario.
- He has a condition known as arthrogryposis, which affects his lower limbs and muscle development.
