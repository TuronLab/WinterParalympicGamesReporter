# Lauren Parker: A Trailblazer in Paralympic Sports

## Introduction
Lauren Parker is an Australian Paralympic athlete specializing in para-biathlon, para-triathlon, and para-cycling. Born on December 15, 1988, in Newcastle, New South Wales, Parker transitioned to para-sports after a serious training accident in April 2017. She will compete in the 2026 Winter Paralympics.

## Career Overview
Lauren Parker's career trajectory includes her transition into para-sports after her accident in 2017. She initially focused on para-triathlon and expanded her repertoire to include para-cycling and para-biathlon.

## Major Achievements
- **Para-Triathlon**:
  - Silver medal in Women's PTWC at the 2020 Summer Paralympics.
  - Gold medal in Women's PTWC at the 2024 Summer Paralympics.
  
- **Para-Cycling**:
  - Gold medal in cycling at the 2024 Summer Paralympics.
  - Silver medal in cycling at the 2024 Summer Paralympics.

- **Para-Biathlon**:
  - Competing in para-biathlon at the 2026 Winter Paralympics.
