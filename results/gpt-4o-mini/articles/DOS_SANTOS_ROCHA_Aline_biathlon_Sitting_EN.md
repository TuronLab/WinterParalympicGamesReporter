# Aline DOS SANTOS ROCHA: A Trailblazer in Brazilian Winter Sports

## Introduction
Aline DOS SANTOS ROCHA is a Brazilian athlete specializing in biathlon and athletics. Aline is recognized as a pioneer in Brazilian winter sports, having made significant contributions to the visibility and representation of athletes with disabilities in her country.

## Career Overview
Aline's athletic career began in basketball before she transitioned to winter sports. She became paraplegic after a car accident at the age of 15. This transition led her to explore various sports, ultimately finding her niche in both athletics and biathlon.

- **Basketball**: Initial sport before transitioning to winter sports.
- **Biathlon**: Competed in multiple Paralympic Winter Games.
- **Athletics**: Achieved notable success in the 400m T53/T54 category.

## Major Achievements
- **Biathlon**:
  - Represented Brazil in the 2022 Beijing Winter Paralympics.
  - Set to carry the Brazilian flag for the third time at the 2026 Paralympic Winter Games, marking a record for Brazil.

- **Athletics**:
  - Silver medal in the 400m T53/T54 at the Parapan American Games (2023).

## Paralympic Participations
- **2018 Paralympic Winter Games**: Competed in biathlon.
- **2022 Paralympic Winter Games**: Competed in biathlon.
- **2026 Paralympic Winter Games**: Scheduled to compete in biathlon and will carry the Brazilian flag.

## Other Sports
- **Athletics**:
  - Silver medal in the 400m T53/T54 at the Parapan American Games (2023).

## Notable Facts
- Aline became paraplegic after a car accident at age 15, which significantly influenced her sports career trajectory.
- She has made history by being the first Brazilian athlete to carry the national flag at the Paralympic Winter Games on three occasions.
