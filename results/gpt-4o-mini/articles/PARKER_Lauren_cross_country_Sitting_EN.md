# Lauren Parker: A Trailblazer in Paralympic Winter Sports

## Introduction
Lauren Parker OAM is an accomplished Australian athlete specializing in cross-country skiing and para-biathlon. Born on December 15, 1988, Parker competes in the LW10 classification for Para Nordic Skiing. 

## Career Overview
Lauren Parker's athletic career began in triathlon, where she represented Australia at a national level. In April 2017, a serious training accident resulted in paraplegia. Following her recovery, she transitioned to para-triathlon and has since expanded her repertoire to include para-cycling and cross-country skiing. In June 2025, Parker made the switch to cross-country skiing, preparing for her debut in the sport at the upcoming 2026 Winter Paralympics.

## Major Achievements
- **Para-Triathlon**:
  - Silver medal in Women's PTWC at the 2020 Summer Paralympics (Tokyo).
  - Gold medals in Women's PTWC for both triathlon and cycling at the 2024 Summer Paralympics (Paris).
- **Para-Cycling**:
  - Gold medal in Women's Time Trial H3 at the 2023 UCI Para-cycling Road World Championships in Glasgow.
  - Silver medal in Women's Road Race H3 at the 2023 UCI Para-cycling Road World Championships in Glasgow.
  - Gold medal in Women's Road Race H1-4 at the 2024 Summer Paralympics (Paris).
  - Silver medal in Women's Road Time Trial H1-3 at the 2024 Summer Paralympics (Paris).
- **Cross-Country Skiing**:
  - Scheduled to compete in para-biathlon and cross-country skiing at the 2026 Winter Paralympics.
