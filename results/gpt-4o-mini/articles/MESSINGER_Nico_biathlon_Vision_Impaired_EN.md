# Nico Messinger: A Rising Star in Paralympic Biathlon

## Introduction
Nico Messinger is a biathlete from Germany. He competes in the men's classification for athletes with vision impairment, specifically classified as LW10.

## Career Overview
Nico Messinger has seen success in various international competitions, including podium finishes in World Cup events and World Championships. He has also competed in para cross-country skiing, where he has achieved a silver medal.

## Major Achievements
- **Biathlon:**
  - **Overall World Cup Winner** in Men's Biathlon with vision impairment, 2025 season [Source: Team Deutschland Paralympics].
  - **Silver medal** in the 10 km race at the Ski Nordic World Championships, 2023 [Source: drs.org].
  - **1st place** in the Individual race at Para Biathlon Heimweltcup over 12.5 kilometers, 9th January 2026 [Source: Team Deutschland Paralympics].
  - **2nd place** in the 10 km race at the Ski Nordic World Championships, 26th January 2023 [Source: drs.org].
  
- **Para Cross-Country Skiing:**
  - **Silver medal** in the sprint at the Ski Nordic World Championships, 2023 [Source: drs.org].

## Performance Trends
- Maintains a pattern of consistent performances, evidenced by multiple podium finishes in recent competitions.
- Showcased high shooting accuracy, successfully hitting 19 out of 20 shots in recent races [Source: drs.org].

## Paralympic Participations
- **2018 PyeongChang Winter Paralympics:** Competed [Source: Team Deutschland Paralympics].
- **2022 Beijing Winter Paralympics:** Competed [Source: Team Deutschland Paralympics].

## Other Sports
- **Para Cross-Country Skiing**:
  - Achieved a **silver medal** in sprint events at the Ski Nordic World Championships, 2023 [Source: drs.org].

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Nico Messinger emphasizes both physical fitness and shooting accuracy. His preparation involves a support team, including his guide, Robin Wunderle, who assists him during competitions [Source: Team Deutschland Paralympics].

## Notable Facts
- Age: 31 (As of 2026).
- Origin: Freiburg, Germany [Source: Team Deutschland Paralympics].
