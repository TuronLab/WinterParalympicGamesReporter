# Carina Edlingerová: A Rising Star in Paralympic Biathlon

## Introduction
Carina Edlingerová, born on August 13, 1998, is a Czech athlete specializing in biathlon, competing in the visually impaired category (B2). She transitioned to the Czech team in 2025. 

## Career Overview
Carina Edlingerová's career began in cross-country skiing, where she gained recognition for her talents. She subsequently transitioned to para biathlon. 

## Major Achievements
Carina Edlingerová has made a substantial mark in her sport, as evidenced by her impressive list of accomplishments:

- **World Championships:**
  - Gold at the 2025 World Championships in Pokljuka (3 medals).
  - Bronze at the 2026 Paralympic Trials in Val di Fiemme.
  
- **Paralympic Games:**
  - Bronze medal in women's 7.5 km classical cross-country skiing at the 2018 Winter Paralympics.
  - Gold medal in the 1.5 km sprint and Bronze in the 10 km free at the 2022 Winter Paralympics.

## Paralympic Participations
Carina Edlingerová has participated in several Paralympic events, specifically:

- **2018 Winter Paralympics**: 
  - Sport: Cross-country skiing
  - Result: Bronze medal in the women's 7.5 km classical event
  
- **2022 Winter Paralympics**: 
  - Sport: Biathlon
  - Result: Gold medal in the 1.5 km sprint and Bronze in the 10 km free

## Other Sports
Before focusing on biathlon, Carina Edlingerová was actively involved in cross-country skiing, achieving significant success:

- **World Championships Titles**: 6 titles in Nordic skiing.
- **World Cup Success**: Overall World Cup winner in the 2016/2017 and 2017/2018 seasons.

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Carina Edlingerová engages in regular training with the Czech para biathlon team, focusing on both the skiing and shooting elements essential for success in biathlon. Specific training methodologies and techniques are not publicly available at this time.

## Notable Facts
- Carina Edlingerová has Stargardt disease, a hereditary condition that leads to progressive vision loss.
- Her decision to switch national representation arose from disagreements with the Austrian Ski Association regarding their support.
