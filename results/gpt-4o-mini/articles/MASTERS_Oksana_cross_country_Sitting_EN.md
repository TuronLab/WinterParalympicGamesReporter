# Oksana Masters: A Trailblazer in Paralympic Winter Sports

## Introduction
Oksana Masters is an American athlete specializing in cross-country skiing, competing in the sitting classification (LW12). She has participated in multiple Paralympic Games since 2012.

## Career Overview
Oksana Masters began her athletic career in para-rowing and para-cycling before transitioning to cross-country skiing. Her first major appearances in cross-country skiing were in the 2018 Pyeongchang Paralympics. She has excelled in her sport, culminating in her performance at the Beijing 2022 Paralympics.

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing:**
  - Gold in 12.5km Individual - Sitting - Women (Beijing 2022)
  - Gold in 4 x 2.5km Relay Mixed (Beijing 2022)
  - Gold in 6km Individual - Sitting - Women (Beijing 2022)
  - Silver in 10km Middle - Sitting - Women (Beijing 2022)
  - Silver in 15km - Sitting - Women (Beijing 2022)
  - Silver in 1km Sprint - Sitting - Women (Beijing 2022)
  - Silver in 7.5km - Sitting - Women (Beijing 2022)
  - Gold in 1km Sprint - Sitting - Women (Pyeongchang 2018)
  - Gold in 5km - Sitting - Women (Pyeongchang 2018)
  - Silver in 12.5km Individual - Sitting - Women (Pyeongchang 2018)
  - Bronze in 12km - Sitting - Women (Pyeongchang 2018)

- **Overall Achievements:**
  - Most decorated U.S. Winter Paralympian of all time with 14 medals.
  - First American to win seven medals at a single Paralympic Games (Beijing 2022).
  - 19-time Paralympic medalist (9 gold, 7 silver, 3 bronze).

## Performance Trends
- Consistent medalist in major events, particularly at the Beijing 2022 Paralympics, where she won multiple medals.

## Notable Facts
