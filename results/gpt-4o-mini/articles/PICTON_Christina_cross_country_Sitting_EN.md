# Christina Picton: A Trailblazer in Paralympic Winter Sports

## Introduction
Christina Picton is a Canadian Paralympic athlete specializing in cross-country skiing and biathlon. Born on April 6, 1993, in St. Catharines, Ontario, she competes in the LW12 classification for sitting athletes.

## Career Overview
Christina Picton has been a member of the Canada Women's National Ice Sledge Hockey Team since 2006. She transitioned to para nordic skiing in December 2018, quickly progressing to provincial team status by 2019.

## Major Achievements
- **2022 Winter Paralympics (Beijing)**:
  - 6th in Cross-Country Sprint Sitting
  - 7th in Biathlon Sprint Sitting
  - 7th in Biathlon Individual Sitting
  - 8th in Biathlon Middle Distance Sitting
- **2023 World Para Nordic Ski Championships (Östersund)**:
  - 3rd place in Biathlon Individual 12.5 km
- **Para Biathlon World Cup**:
  - 4th in the 12.5 km individual at the Para Biathlon World Cup circuit in January 2026
  - Multiple top-five finishes in World Cups from 2021 onward
  - 3 top-five finishes at the 2021-22 season opening World Cup in Canmore
- **Para Ice Hockey**:
  - Silver medal at the inaugural Women's Para Ice Hockey World Championships in 2025

## Performance Trends
- Consistent top-eight finishes in major competitions since her debut.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2022 Winter Paralympics (Beijing)**:
  - Cross-Country Skiing: 6th in Sprint Sitting
  - Biathlon: 7th in Sprint Sitting, 7th in Individual Sitting, 8th in Middle Distance Sitting

## Other Sports
- **Para Ice Hockey**:
  - Member of the Canada Women's National Ice Sledge Hockey Team since 2006
  - Captain during the 2014-15 season
  - First woman to try out for Hockey Canada's senior men's para ice hockey team in 2019
  - Led Canada to silver at the inaugural Women's Para Ice Hockey World Championships in 2025

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Christina Picton discovered para nordic skiing through a participant in her ice hockey program. She progressed quickly from her first race to earning provincial team status in 2019.

## Notable Facts
- Christina Picton was born with congenital femoral deficiency, which led to the amputation of her right leg at age 16.
