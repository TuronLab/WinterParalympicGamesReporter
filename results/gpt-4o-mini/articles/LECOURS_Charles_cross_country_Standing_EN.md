# Charles Lecours: A Rising Star in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Charles Lecours is a Canadian athlete specializing in cross-country skiing, competing in the LW9 (ST - Standing) classification. Born on September 8, 2001, Lecours has emerged as a notable figure in the world of Paralympic sports, particularly in cross-country skiing.

## Career Overview
Charles Lecours began his competitive journey in cross-country skiing, participating in both World Cup and Continental Cup events. Notably, he achieved a milestone by becoming the first Para Cross-Country Skiing FISU medalist for Canada.

## Major Achievements
- **1st Place** in the men's 4km Para standing race at the Eastern Canada Cup (December 18, 2022) [Source: Nordiq Canada].
- **First Para Cross-Country Skiing FISU medalist for Canada** (January 17, 2025) [Source: Facebook post].
- **18th Place** in Mass Start at Canmore (December 7, 2025) [Source: FIS].
- **18th Place** in Sprint Final at Canmore (December 6, 2025) [Source: FIS].
- **20th Place** in Interval Start at Canmore (December 4, 2025) [Source: FIS].
- Multiple podium finishes in Continental Cup events.
