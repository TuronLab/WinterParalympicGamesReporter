# Oleksandr Aleksyk: A Rising Star in Paralympic Winter Sports

## Introduction
Oleksandr Aleksyk is a Ukrainian athlete specializing in cross-country skiing and biathlon, competing in the B1 classification for visually impaired athletes. Born on July 23, 2004, Aleksyk has participated in the Winter Paralympics.

## Career Overview
Aleksyk's career in winter sports has included participation in both cross-country skiing and biathlon events. He debuted at the 2018 Winter Paralympics and continued to compete at the 2022 Winter Paralympics.

### Sports and Categories Participated In:
- **Cross-Country Skiing**: B1 classification (visually impaired)
- **Biathlon**: B1 classification (visually impaired)

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing**:
  - Participated in the 2022 Paralympic Winter Games.
    - 11th place in Men's 7.5 km - Sitting (12 March 2022)
    - 13th place in Men's 15 km - Sitting (11 March 2022)
    - 13th place in Men's 12.5 km - Sitting (8 March 2022)
    - 19th place in Men's 7.5 km - Sitting (5 March 2022)

- **Biathlon**:
  - Gold medal in Men's 12.5 km visually impaired biathlon at the 2022 Winter Paralympics.
  - Silver medal in Men's 6 km visually impaired biathlon at the 2022 Winter Paralympics.
  - Silver medal in Men's 12.5 km visually impaired biathlon at the 2018 Winter Paralympics.
  - Bronze medal in Men's 6 km visually impaired biathlon at the 2021 World Para Snow Sports Championships.
