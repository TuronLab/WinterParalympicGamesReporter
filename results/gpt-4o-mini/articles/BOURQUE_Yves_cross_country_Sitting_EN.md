# Yves Bourque: A Journey Through Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Yves Bourque is a Canadian athlete specializing in cross-country skiing, competing in the sitting classification. He has represented Canada in two Paralympic Winter Games. Born without legs, Bourque has been involved in para-nordic skiing since 2008.

## Career Overview
Yves Bourque's career in cross-country skiing began in earnest in 2008, leading to his debut at the Paralympic Winter Games in 2014. His journey has been marked by consistent participation in major events, including:

- **2008**: Began competitive para-nordic skiing.
- **2014**: Competed in the Sochi Paralympic Winter Games.
- **2018**: Competed in the Pyeongchang Paralympic Winter Games.

Throughout his career, Bourque has primarily focused on cross-country skiing, although he has also participated in snowboarding.

## Major Achievements
- **2014 Paralympic Winter Games**:
  - Men's 15 km Sitting: 20th
  - Men's 1 km Sprint Sitting: 24th
  - Men's 10 km Sitting: 24th
- **2018 Paralympic Winter Games**:
  - Men's 15 km Sitting: 25th
  - Men's 1.1 km Sprint Sitting: 29th
  - Men's 7.5 km Sitting: 31st
- Competed in national championships, achieving personal bests leading up to the Paralympics.
