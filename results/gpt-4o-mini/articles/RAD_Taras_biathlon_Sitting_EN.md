# Taras Rad: A Rising Star in Paralympic Biathlon

## Introduction
Taras Rad is a Ukrainian Paralympic athlete specializing in biathlon, competing in the LW12 classification for sitting athletes. He debuted at the 2018 Winter Paralympics, where he won a gold medal in Men's 12.5 km Sitting. Rad's journey in sports began after a leg injury at the age of 14, which led to an amputation.

## Career Overview
Taras Rad's career in biathlon began with his participation in the 2018 Winter Paralympics, where he won a gold medal. His trajectory includes:

- **2018**: Debuted at the PyeongChang Winter Paralympics, winning a gold medal in Men's 12.5 km Sitting.
- **2022**: Competed at the Beijing Winter Paralympics, where he earned a silver in Men's Sprint Sitting and bronzes in Men's 6 km and Men's 10 km Sitting events.

In addition to biathlon, Rad has also participated in cross-country skiing, swimming, and previously in football and athletics before his amputation.

## Major Achievements
- **Biathlon**:
  - Gold medal in Men's 12.5 km Sitting at PyeongChang 2018.
  - Silver medal in Men's Sprint Sitting at Beijing 2022.
  - Bronze medals in Men's 6 km Sitting and Men's 10 km Sitting at Beijing 2022.
  - Multiple medals at the 2017 World Para Nordic Skiing Championships, including three silver medals.
