# Marco Maier: A Rising Star in Paralympic Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Marco Maier is a distinguished German athlete specializing in cross-country skiing and biathlon. Born on December 20, 1999, he competes in the LW8 classification, which is designated for standing athletes.

## Career Overview
Marco Maier's career in competitive skiing includes participation in both cross-country skiing and biathlon, with notable achievements in each discipline. 

- **2022**: Competed in the Winter Paralympics, earning a silver medal in the 1.5 km sprint and a silver medal in the 6 km standing biathlon event.
- **2023**: Achieved success at the World Para Nordic Skiing Championships, winning gold in both the sprint freestyle and the 4x2.5 km open relay.
- **2025**: Secured gold medals in the 10 km freestyle and sprint classical events at the Winter World University Games.

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing**:
  - Silver medal at the 2022 Winter Paralympics (1.5 km sprint).
  - Gold medals at the 2023 World Championships (sprint freestyle, 4x2.5 km relay).
  - Gold medal at the 2025 Winter University Games (10 km freestyle).
  - Gold medal at the 2025 Winter University Games (sprint classical).

- **Biathlon**:
  - Silver medal at the 2022 Winter Paralympics (6 km standing).
  - Gold medal at the 2023 World Championships (7.5 km standing).

## Paralympic Participations
- **2022 Winter Paralympics**:
  - Sport: Cross-Country Skiing
    - Result: Silver in 1.5 km sprint
  - Sport: Biathlon
    - Result: Silver in 6 km standing

- **Upcoming**: Preparing for the 2026 Winter Paralympics.

## Other Sports
- **Biathlon**:
  - Silver medal at the 2022 Winter Paralympics (6 km standing).
  - Gold medal at the 2023 World Championships (7.5 km standing).

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Marco Maier's training regimen focuses on both cross-country skiing and biathlon. He has tailored his preparation to accommodate the classic style of skiing, particularly due to equipment limitations that require the use of only one pole. His training is guided by coach Ralf Rombach.

## Notable Facts
- Marco was born with symbrachydactyly, which affects three fingers on his left hand.
- He is a professional athlete and a member of the Zoll Ski Team.
