# Andrea Eskau: A Trailblazer in Paralympic Winter Sports

## Introduction
Andrea Eskau is a German athlete, born on March 21, 1971, who competes in cross-country skiing and handcycling. Competing in the sitting classification (H4) due to a spinal cord injury, Eskau has participated in multiple Paralympic Games.

## Career Overview
Andrea Eskau's athletic career began after she became a paraplegic due to a cycling accident in 1998. She started competing in 2002. Her career includes participation in both winter and summer Paralympic events, with notable achievements in cross-country skiing and handcycling.

### Sports and Categories Participated In:
- **Cross-Country Skiing**: Sitting (H4)
- **Handcycling**: Various events including Road Race and Time Trial

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing**:
  - **2018 PyeongChang**: 
    - Gold - 10 km Biathlon
    - Gold - 12.5 km Biathlon
    - Silver - 12 km Sitting
    - Silver - 1.1 km Sprint Sitting
    - Silver - 5 km Sitting
    - Bronze - Mixed Relay (4x2.5 km)
  - **2014 Sochi**: 
    - Gold - 6 km Biathlon
    - Gold - 5 km Cross-Country
  - **2010 Vancouver**: 
    - Bronze - 10 km Biathlon
    - Silver - 5 km Cross-Country

- **Handcycling**:
  - **Summer Paralympics**:
    - Gold - Road Race H5 (2016 Rio de Janeiro)
    - Gold - Time Trial (2012 London)
    - Gold - Road Race (2012 London)
    - Gold - Road Race (2008 Beijing)
  - **World Championships**: 
    - Gold in Road Race and Time Trial (2017 UCI Paracycling World Championships)
