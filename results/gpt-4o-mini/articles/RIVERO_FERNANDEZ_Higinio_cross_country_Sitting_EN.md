# Higinio Rivero Fernandez: A Rising Star in Paralympic Winter Sports

## Introduction
Higinio Rivero Fernandez is a Spanish athlete specializing in cross-country skiing and biathlon, classified under the LW10.5 category (sitting). He is set to compete in the Milano-Cortina 2026 Paralympic Games.

## Career Overview
Higinio's journey in adaptive sports began after he suffered a spinal injury in 2013. He has since participated in various international competitions.

## Major Achievements
- **Cross-Country Skiing:**
  - Qualified for the Milano-Cortina 2026 Paralympic Games in both biathlon and cross-country skiing.

- **Paracanoeing:**
  - Competed in the Tokyo 2020 Paralympics, finishing sixth.
  - Achieved a World silver medal in paracanoeing in 2021.

## Performance Trends
- Higinio has shown gradual improvement in his rankings, particularly during the 2025-2026 season, participating in multiple World Cup events.

## Paralympic Participations
- **Milano-Cortina 2026 Paralympic Games**
  - Sport: Cross-Country Skiing and Biathlon

## Other Sports
- **Paracanoeing:**
  - Competed in the Tokyo 2020 Paralympics, finishing sixth.
  - World silver medallist in paracanoeing (2021).

## Preparation Style / Training Approach
Higinio utilizes an ergometer for ski training and practices shooting techniques under fatigue conditions. He trains in various locations including Italy, Slovenia, and France.

## Notable Facts
- Born on March 19, 1982, in Bilbao, Spain.
- Represents the Club Pirineísta Mayencos and the Federación Aragonesa de Deportes de Invierno (FADI).
