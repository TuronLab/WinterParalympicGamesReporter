# Jesse Bachinsky: A Beacon of Resilience and Achievement in Cross-Country Skiing

## Introduction
Jesse Bachinsky is a prominent Canadian athlete in cross-country skiing. Born on May 26, 1998, in Kenora, Ontario, he competes in the visually impaired (VI) classification. 

## Career Overview
Jesse's career in cross-country skiing began in local races around 2017. He aims to compete at the 2026 Milano Cortina Paralympics.

## Major Achievements
- **2019 Canada Winter Games**
  - Gold Medal: Sprint
  - Bronze Medal: Middle Distance
  - Bronze Medal: Long Distance
- **2019 World Para Nordic Skiing Championships**
  - 10th Place: Men's Long Distance - Classic VI
  - 11th Place: Men's Sprint - Free Style VI
  - 15th Place: Men's Middle Distance - Free Style VI
- **Canadian Ski Nationals 2015**
  - 2 Gold Medals, 1 Silver Medal
- **2019 Manitoba Skier of the Year**: Recognized for outstanding performance throughout the season.

## Performance Trends
- Transitioned from local competitions to World Cup events since 2017.
- Maintained consistent top 15 rankings at World Para Nordic skiing events.
- Significant improvement noted in recent competitive seasons.

## Paralympic Participations
- **2024-25 Season**: 
  - 8th Place: 10 km at Paralympic Games test event in Val di Fiemme, Italy.
- Aims to participate in the **2026 Milano Cortina Paralympics**.

## Other Sports
- **Snowboarding**: Works as an instructor during off-seasons from Nordic skiing.
- Engages in various summer activities including kayaking, paddle boarding, and canoeing; however, detailed achievements in these areas are not available.

## Training Style / Preparation Approach
Jesse trains with a guide, typically Levi Nadlersmith, who communicates via a radio system during races.

## Notable Facts
Jesse was born with no central vision and was diagnosed with a degenerative eye disease that led to complete blindness by the age of 15.
