# Taras Rad: A Profile of Resilience and Achievement in Paralympic Sports

## Introduction
Taras Rad is a Ukrainian athlete specializing in cross-country skiing and biathlon. Born on November 20, 1999, he competes in the LW12 classification, which is designated for sitting athletes. 

## Career Overview
Taras Rad had his leg amputated due to an infection from an untreated injury at age 14. He transitioned to adaptive sports through the Invasport Para sports organization in Ukraine. Rad has developed a career in both cross-country skiing and biathlon.

### Sports and Categories Participated
- **Cross-Country Skiing**: LW12 (Sitting)
- **Biathlon**: LW12 (Sitting)

## Major Achievements
- **PyeongChang 2018 Paralympic Winter Games**:
  - Gold in Men's 12.5km Sitting (Cross-Country Skiing)
  - 4th in Men's 7.5km Sitting (Cross-Country Skiing)
  - 5th in Men's 1.1km Sprint Sitting (Cross-Country Skiing)
  - Gold in Men's Biathlon Middle Distance Sitting (Biathlon)
  
- **Prince George 2019 World Para Nordic Skiing Championships**:
  - Gold in Men's Sprint Sitting (Cross-Country Skiing)
  - Gold in Men's Long Distance Sitting (Cross-Country Skiing)
  - Silver in Men's Long Distance - Classic Sitting (Cross-Country Skiing)

- **Lillehammer 2021 World Para Snow Sports Championships**:
  - Silver in Men's Middle Distance Sitting (Cross-Country Skiing)
  - Bronze in Men's Sprint Sitting (Cross-Country Skiing)
